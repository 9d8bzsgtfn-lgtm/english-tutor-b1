Tuesday Vocabulary Podcast: Travel & Tourism

TEMA DEL DÍA

Vocabulario relacionado con viajes, turismo y vacaciones.

Nivel: B1 | Duración estimada: 10-15 minutos

Podcast Script

INTRO:

"Hello and welcome to Tuesday Vocabulary! Today we're going to learn vocabulary about travel and tourism. Whether you're planning a holiday or just dreaming about one, this vocabulary will be very useful. Let's get started!"

SECTION 1: Types of Holidays

"First, let's learn different types of holidays. Listen and repeat."

package holiday /ˈpækɪdʒ ˈhɒlɪdeɪ/ viaje organizado

"We booked a package holiday to Greece - it includes flights and hotel."

backpacking /ˈbækpækɪŋ/ mochilero

"I went backpacking around Southeast Asia for three months."

city break /ˈsɪti breɪk/ escapada urbana

"We had a lovely city break in Barcelona last weekend."

cruise /kruːz/ crucero

"My parents went on a cruise around the Mediterranean."

road trip /rəʊd trɪp/ viaje por carretera

"We're planning a road trip along the coast."

SECTION 2: Accommodation

"Now let's learn vocabulary for places to stay."

bed and breakfast (B&B) /bed ənd ˈbrekfəst/ pensión con desayuno

"We stayed at a lovely B&B in the countryside."

hostel /ˈhɒstəl/ albergue

"Hostels are a cheap option for young travellers."

self-catering /self ˈkeɪtərɪŋ/ con cocina propia

"We rented a self-catering apartment with a kitchen."

all-inclusive /ɔːl ɪnˈkluːsɪv/ todo incluido

"The all-inclusive resort includes all meals and drinks."

vacancy / no vacancy /ˈveɪkənsi/ habitaciones libres / completo

"The sign said 'No Vacancy' - the hotel was full."

SECTION 3: At the Airport

"Let's learn essential airport vocabulary."

boarding pass /ˈbɔːdɪŋ pɑːs/ tarjeta de embarque

"Please have your boarding pass ready."

check in /tʃek ɪn/ facturar

"You can check in online 24 hours before your flight."

hand luggage / carry-on /hænd ˈlʌɡɪdʒ/ equipaje de mano

"You can only bring one piece of hand luggage."

departure lounge /dɪˈpɑːtʃə laʊndʒ/ sala de embarque

"We waited in the departure lounge for two hours."

layover / stopover /ˈleɪəʊvə/ escala

"We had a four-hour layover in Dubai."

SECTION 4: Sightseeing

"Finally, vocabulary for exploring new places."

landmark /ˈlændmɑːk/ monumento, lugar emblemático

"The Eiffel Tower is Paris's most famous landmark."

guided tour /ˈɡaɪdɪd tʊə/ visita guiada

"We took a guided tour of the museum."

souvenir /suːvəˈnɪə/ recuerdo

"I bought some souvenirs for my family."

off the beaten track /ɒf ðə ˈbiːtn træk/ fuera de lo común

"I prefer places that are off the beaten track."

breathtaking /ˈbreθteɪkɪŋ/ impresionante

"The view from the mountain was breathtaking."

OUTRO:

"Excellent! Now you have lots of vocabulary for your next trip. Remember, the best way to learn is to use these words when you travel or talk about travelling. See you tomorrow for Wednesday's vocabulary podcast. Safe travels and goodbye!"

Practice Exercises

EJERCICIO 1: Match the definitions
  1. A holiday where flights and hotel are included together
  2. A famous building or place that everyone knows
  3. A place where you sleep in shared rooms cheaply
  4. The document you need to get on the plane
  5. When you stop in another city during your journey

Words: landmark, boarding pass, package holiday, hostel, layover

ANSWERS
  1. package holiday
  2. landmark
  3. hostel
  4. boarding pass
  5. layover
SPEAKING PRACTICE

Use today's vocabulary to answer these questions:

  1. What type of holiday do you prefer - package holidays, backpacking, or city breaks?
  2. Describe the most breathtaking place you've ever visited.
  3. Do you prefer all-inclusive resorts or self-catering accommodation?
