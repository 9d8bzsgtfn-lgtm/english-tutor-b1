Monday Vocabulary Podcast: Work & Career

TEMA DEL DÍA

Vocabulario relacionado con el trabajo, profesiones y la vida laboral.

Nivel: B1 | Duración estimada: 10-15 minutos

Podcast Script

INTRO:

"Hello and welcome to Monday Vocabulary! I'm your English tutor, and today we're going to learn vocabulary about work and careers. This is essential vocabulary for job interviews, talking about your profession, and everyday work conversations. Let's begin!"

SECTION 1: Job Titles and Professions

"First, let's learn some common job titles. Listen and repeat after me."

accountant /əˈkaʊntənt/ contable

"My sister works as an accountant for a big company."

entrepreneur /ˌɒntrəprəˈnɜː/ emprendedor

"He's an entrepreneur who started his own tech company."

freelancer /ˈfriːlɑːnsə/ autónomo, freelance

"She's a freelancer, so she works from home."

manager /ˈmænɪdʒə/ gerente, director

"I need to speak to the manager about this problem."

colleague /ˈkɒliːɡ/ compañero de trabajo

"My colleagues are very friendly and helpful."

SECTION 2: Work Verbs

"Now, let's learn important verbs related to work."

apply (for) /əˈplaɪ/ solicitar

"I'm going to apply for a new job next week."

hire /ˈhaɪə/ contratar

"The company wants to hire five new employees."

resign /rɪˈzaɪn/ dimitir

"He resigned from his job to travel the world."

promote /prəˈməʊt/ ascender

"She was promoted to senior manager last month."

retire /rɪˈtaɪə/ jubilarse

"My father is going to retire next year."

SECTION 3: Workplace Vocabulary

"Let's learn words for things and places at work."

salary /ˈsæləri/ salario

"The salary for this position is very competitive."

deadline /ˈdedlaɪn/ fecha límite

"The deadline for this project is Friday."

meeting /ˈmiːtɪŋ/ reunión

"We have a meeting with the client at 10 o'clock."

overtime /ˈəʊvətaɪm/ horas extra

"I had to work overtime to finish the report."

contract /ˈkɒntrækt/ contrato

"I signed a two-year contract with the company."

SECTION 4: Useful Expressions

"Finally, let's learn some common work expressions."

work-life balance /wɜːk laɪf ˈbæləns/ equilibrio trabajo-vida

"I'm trying to improve my work-life balance."

be in charge of /bi ɪn tʃɑːdʒ ɒv/ estar a cargo de

"She's in charge of the marketing department."

make a living /meɪk ə ˈlɪvɪŋ/ ganarse la vida

"He makes a living as a graphic designer."

job satisfaction /dʒɒb sætɪsˈfækʃən/ satisfacción laboral

"Job satisfaction is more important than money to me."

OUTRO:

"Great work today! Remember to review this vocabulary and try to use it in your own sentences. Practice makes perfect! See you tomorrow for Tuesday's vocabulary podcast. Goodbye!"

Practice Exercises

EJERCICIO 1: Fill in the blanks
  1. I need to _________ for a new job because I'm not happy here.
  2. My _________ are very supportive and we work well together.
  3. The company decided to _________ her to regional manager.
  4. I often have to work _________ during busy periods.
  5. He's a _________, so he chooses his own projects and clients.
ANSWERS
  1. apply
  2. colleagues
  3. promote
  4. overtime
  5. freelancer
SPEAKING PRACTICE

Use today's vocabulary to answer these questions:

  1. What do you do for a living?
  2. What's the most important thing for you in a job - salary, job satisfaction, or work-life balance?
  3. Have you ever had to work overtime? How did you feel about it?
