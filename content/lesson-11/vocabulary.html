Vocabulary: Essential Phrasal Verbs for B1

TEMA

Los phrasal verbs más importantes organizados por temas: viajes, relaciones, rutina diaria y trabajo.

Travel & Movement

check in / check out
/tʃek ɪn/ /tʃek aʊt/
registrarse / dejar el hotel
"We need to check out before 11am."
get on / get off
/ɡet ɒn/ /ɡet ɒf/
subir / bajar (transporte)
"Get off at the next station."
take off
/teɪk ɒf/
despegar (avión)
"The plane takes off at 6pm."
set off / set out
/set ɒf/ /set aʊt/
ponerse en camino, partir
"We set off early to avoid traffic."
pick up
/pɪk ʌp/
recoger (a alguien)
"I'll pick you up at the airport."
drop off
/drɒp ɒf/
dejar (a alguien en un sitio)
"Can you drop me off at the station?"
get back
/ɡet bæk/
volver, regresar
"When did you get back from holiday?"
get away
/ɡet əˈweɪ/
escapar, irse de vacaciones
"I need to get away for a few days."

Relationships & Social

/ɡet ɒn wɪð/

get along with
/ɡet əˈlɒŋ wɪð/

/fɔːl aʊt/

/meɪk ʌp/

/breɪk ʌp/

/ɑːsk aʊt/

look after
/lʊk ˈɑːftə/

take after
/teɪk ˈɑːftə/

"She takes after her grandmother."

Common Expressions

EXPRESIONES FRECUENTES
Expression Meaning Example
run out of quedarse sin We've run out of coffee.
look forward to esperar con ilusión I look forward to hearing from you.
get rid of deshacerse de I need to get rid of these old clothes.
come across encontrar por casualidad I came across an old friend yesterday.
give up rendirse, abandonar Never give up on your dreams.
keep up with mantenerse al día It's hard to keep up with technology.
PRACTICA

Practica con el tutor usando phrasal verbs:

  • "What time do you usually get up?"
  • "Do you get on well with your neighbours?"
  • "Have you ever turned down a job offer?"
