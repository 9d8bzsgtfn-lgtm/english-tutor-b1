Grammar: Future Forms

IMPRESCINDIBLE - MEMORIZAR

Las 4 formas de futuro en inglés

Forma Uso principal Ejemplo
WILL Decisiones espontáneas, predicciones, promesas I'll help you.
GOING TO Planes/intenciones, predicciones con evidencia I'm going to study medicine.
Present Continuous Planes fijos/organizados I'm meeting John at 5.
Present Simple Horarios, programas fijos The train leaves at 9.

Estructura gramatical

WILL GOING TO
+ I/You/He/She/It/We/They will work
(contracción: I'll, you'll, he'll...) 		+ I am going to work
+ He/She/It is going to work
+ You/We/They are going to work
- I/You/He... will not (won't) work - I 'm not going to work
- He isn't going to work
? Will you work? ? Are you going to work?

WILL vs GOING TO - La diferencia clave

MUY IMPORTANTE PARA EXAMEN
WILL GOING TO
Decisión espontánea (ahora mismo) Plan previo (ya decidido antes)
"The phone's ringing." - "I'll answer it." "I'm going to visit my parents this weekend." (ya planeado)
Predicción (opinión, sin evidencia) Predicción (con evidencia visible)
"I think it will rain tomorrow." "Look at those clouds! It's going to rain."
Promesas y ofertas Intenciones
"I'll call you later, I promise." "I'm going to learn Japanese next year."

Present Continuous para futuro

PLANES FIJOS

Usamos Present Continuous para planes ya organizados con fecha/hora específica:

  • I'm meeting Sarah at 6pm. (ya quedamos)
  • We're flying to London tomorrow. (billetes comprados)
  • They're getting married in June. (fecha fijada)

Diferencia con GOING TO:

  • I'm going to meet Sarah. = intención, plan general
  • I'm meeting Sarah at 6. = cita confirmada

Present Simple para futuro

HORARIOS Y PROGRAMAS

Usamos Present Simple para horarios oficiales y programas:

  • The train leaves at 9:15.
  • The film starts at 8pm.
  • School finishes on June 20th.

Expresiones con WILL

EXPRESIONES COMUNES
Expresión Uso Ejemplo
I think... will Opinión/predicción I think she'll pass the exam.
I'm sure... will Certeza I'm sure it'll be fine.
probably will Probabilidad He'll probably be late.
Shall I/we...? Ofrecer/sugerir Shall I help you?

Errores comunes a evitar

ERRORES FRECUENTES
Incorrecto Correcto Por qué
I will to help you. I will help you. WILL + infinitivo sin TO
I'm go to visit them. I'm going to visit them. GOING TO, no GO TO
The train will leaves at 9. The train leaves at 9. Horarios = Present Simple
I'll meet you tomorrow. (cita fija) I'm meeting you tomorrow. Cita organizada = Present Continuous
RESUMEN "SUBE LAS ANTENAS"
  • WILL: decisiones espontáneas, promesas, predicciones (opinión)
  • GOING TO: planes/intenciones previas, predicciones con evidencia
  • Present Continuous: citas y planes organizados con fecha
  • Present Simple: horarios oficiales (tren, avión, cine...)
