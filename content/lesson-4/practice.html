Practice: Future Forms

INSTRUCCIONES

Elige la forma correcta del futuro para cada situación.

Exercise 1: WILL or GOING TO?

1. "I haven't got any money." - "Don't worry, I ________ you some."

'll lend
'm going to lend

Correct! This is a spontaneous decision (deciding now to help), so we use WILL.

2. I've decided. I ________ learn to play the guitar this year.

will
'm going to

Correct! "I've decided" shows this is a prior intention/plan, so we use GOING TO.

3. Look at those dark clouds! It ________ rain.

will
's going to

Correct! We can see evidence (dark clouds), so we use GOING TO for the prediction.

4. I think Real Madrid ________ win the match tonight.

will
's going to

Correct! "I think" shows this is an opinion/prediction without evidence, so WILL.

5. "The phone's ringing!" - "I ________ get it."

'll
'm going to

Correct! Spontaneous decision at the moment of speaking = WILL.

Exercise 2: Choose the correct future form

6. The train ________ at 9:15. (timetable)

will leave
is going to leave
leaves

Correct! For timetables and schedules, we use Present Simple.

7. I ________ dinner with Sarah tomorrow at 8. (arranged appointment)

will have
'm having
have

Correct! For fixed arrangements with a specific time, we use Present Continuous.

8. I promise I ________ late again.

won't be
'm not going to be
'm not being

Correct! For promises, we use WILL.

9. ________ I help you with those bags?

Shall
Will
Am I going to

Correct! "Shall I...?" is used to offer help.

10. What time ________ the film ________? (cinema schedule)

will / start
does / start
is / starting

Correct! Cinema schedules are timetables = Present Simple.
