Speaking Practice: Future Forms

INSTRUCCIONES

Practica las diferentes formas de expresar el futuro: Will, Going to, Present Continuous.

Objetivo: Usar la forma correcta del futuro según el contexto y la intención.

Section 1: Predictions (Will)

EXERCISE 1

Make predictions about the future.

Use: I think... will / I don't think... will / ... probably will

Model answer: "I think technology will change a lot in the next 10 years. I think people will work from home more. I don't think books will disappear. Cars will probably become electric. Life will be different."

EXERCISE 2

Make predictions about your own life in 5 years.

Think about: work, home, relationships, hobbies.

Model answer: "In five years, I think I'll have a better job. I'll probably live in a bigger house. Maybe I'll be married. I hope I'll speak English fluently. I definitely won't be in the same position."

EXERCISE 3

Make predictions about the weather, economy, sports.

Examples:

  • "I think it will rain tomorrow."
  • "The economy will probably improve next year."
  • "I don't think they will win the championship."

Section 2: Spontaneous Decisions (Will)

EXERCISE 4

Respond to these situations with a spontaneous decision.

Use: I'll + verb (decision made NOW)

Situations and responses:

  • "The phone is ringing." → "I'll answer it."
  • "I'm thirsty." → "I'll get you some water."
  • "This box is very heavy." → "I'll help you carry it."
  • "I don't understand this." → "I'll explain it to you."
EXERCISE 5

Make offers and promises using "will".

Offers: I'll help you... / Promises: I'll call you...

Practice:

  • "Don't worry, I'll take care of it."
  • "I promise I'll be there on time."
  • "I'll send you the information tomorrow."
  • "I won't tell anyone, I promise."

Section 3: Plans and Intentions (Going to)

EXERCISE 6

Talk about your plans for the weekend.

Use: I'm going to + verb (you've already decided)

Model answer: "This weekend, I'm going to visit my parents. I'm going to do some shopping on Saturday. I'm going to watch a film on Sunday. I'm not going to work at all. I'm going to relax."

EXERCISE 7

Talk about your plans for the next holiday.

Model answer: "For my next holiday, I'm going to go to the beach. I'm going to stay in a nice hotel. I'm going to try local food. I'm not going to check my emails. I'm going to completely disconnect from work."

EXERCISE 8

Talk about your New Year resolutions or life goals.

Use: I'm going to... to express firm intentions.

Model answer: "This year, I'm going to learn a new skill. I'm going to save more money. I'm going to exercise regularly. I'm going to read more books. I'm going to spend more time with family."

Section 4: Predictions Based on Evidence (Going to)

EXERCISE 9

Make predictions based on what you can see or evidence.

Use: It's going to + verb (you have evidence NOW)

Practice:

  • "Look at those clouds! It's going to rain."
  • "She looks very tired. She's going to fall asleep."
  • "The traffic is terrible. We're going to be late."
  • "He's driving too fast. He's going to have an accident."

Section 5: Fixed Arrangements (Present Continuous)

EXERCISE 10

Talk about your appointments and arrangements for this week.

Use: I'm + -ing (arranged, in your diary)

Model answer: "On Monday, I'm having lunch with a client. On Tuesday, I'm meeting my friend at 6. On Wednesday, I'm seeing the dentist. On Thursday, I'm flying to Barcelona. On Friday, I'm having dinner with my family."

EXERCISE 11

Describe your schedule for tomorrow.

Include times: At 9, I'm..., At 12, I'm..., In the evening, I'm...

Section 6: Choosing the Right Future Form

EXERCISE 12

For each situation, decide: Will, Going to, or Present Continuous?

Situations:

  1. You see a friend struggling with bags → "I'll help you."
  2. You made an appointment → "I'm meeting the doctor at 3."
  3. You have a plan to study → "I'm going to study tonight."
  4. You think about the future → "Technology will change."
  5. Dark clouds in the sky → "It's going to rain."
EXERCISE 13

Complete each sentence with the correct future form and explain why:

  • "Look out! You _____ fall!" (evidence)
  • "I think it _____ be a great party." (prediction)
  • "I _____ dinner with Tom tonight." (arrangement)
  • "Wait! I _____ open the door for you." (offer)
  • "We _____ buy a new car." (plan)

Section 7: Shall for Offers and Suggestions

EXERCISE 14

Make offers and suggestions using "Shall I...?" and "Shall we...?"

Practice:

  • "Shall I help you with that?"
  • "Shall I make some coffee?"
  • "Shall we go to the cinema tonight?"
  • "Shall we have lunch together?"
  • "What shall we do this weekend?"

Section 8: Extended Monologue (2 minutes)

EXERCISE 15 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "My plans for the future"

Speak for 2 minutes about your future plans. Include:

  • Your plans for the next few months (going to)
  • Fixed arrangements you have (Present Continuous)
  • Predictions about your life (will)
  • Things you hope/think will happen
EXERCISE 16 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "The world in 2050"

Make predictions about the future of technology, environment, society.

Structure: "I think that in 2050... Cars will probably... People won't... Technology will... The environment will... I hope that..."

EXERCISE 17 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "My career plans"

Discuss your short-term and long-term career plans.

Section 9: Role-Play Scenarios

EXERCISE 18 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Planning a party with a friend

Discuss:

  • "What are we going to do for the party?"
  • "Who shall we invite?"
  • "What time will it start?"
  • "Who's going to bring the food?"
EXERCISE 19 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Making a doctor's appointment

Call to make an appointment. Use future forms naturally.

EXERCISE 20 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Discussing weekend plans with family

Section 10: Quick Response Drills

QUICK DRILL

Respond to these situations quickly:

  1. Someone drops their papers → "I'll..."
  2. You have a flight booked for Friday → "I'm..."
  3. You've decided to learn guitar → "I'm going to..."
  4. You see rain clouds → "It's going to..."
  5. You think about the future → "I think technology will..."
  6. Someone is cold → "Shall I...?"
  7. You want to suggest a restaurant → "Shall we...?"
  8. You promise to call → "I'll..."

Section 11: Spot and Correct the Error

ERROR CORRECTION

Say each sentence correctly:

  1. "I'll going to the cinema tonight." → ?
  2. "Look! The bus will come." (you can see it) → ?
  3. "I'm meet my friend tomorrow." → ?
  4. "Don't worry, I going to help you." → ?
  5. "Shall I to open the window?" → ?
  6. "I think it's going to be sunny." (no evidence) → ?
  7. "Tomorrow I have a meeting." (more formal) → ?
  8. "She will has a baby next month." → ?

Correct answers:

  1. "I'm going to the cinema tonight."
  2. "Look! The bus is coming." / "The bus is going to arrive."
  3. "I'm meeting my friend tomorrow."
  4. "Don't worry, I'm going to help you." / "I'll help you."
  5. "Shall I open the window?"
  6. "I think it will be sunny."
  7. "Tomorrow I'm having a meeting."
  8. "She will have a baby next month." / "She's going to have a baby."
CONSEJO

Recuerda: Will = decisión en el momento o predicción. Going to = plan decidido o evidencia. Present Continuous = cita fija.
