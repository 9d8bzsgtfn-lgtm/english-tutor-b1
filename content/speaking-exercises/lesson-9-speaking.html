Speaking Practice: Reported Speech

INSTRUCCIONES

Practica el estilo indirecto para contar lo que otras personas dijeron, preguntaron o pensaron.

Objetivo: Reportar conversaciones y citas de forma natural y precisa.

Section 1: Reporting Statements

EXERCISE 1

Report what people said. Change the tenses correctly.

Direct: "I am tired." → Reported: He said (that) he was tired.

Practice:

  • "I love this city." → She said she loved that city.
  • "I'm working now." → He said he was working then.
  • "I can help you." → She said she could help me.
  • "I will call later." → He said he would call later.
  • "I have finished." → She said she had finished.
EXERCISE 2

Report what your family members told you recently.

Model answer: "My mother told me she was proud of me. My father said he would visit next week. My sister said she had bought a new car. My brother told me he was looking for a job."

EXERCISE 3

Report news you heard today or this week.

Model answer: "The news said that the economy was improving. They reported that a new law had been passed. The presenter mentioned that the weather would be sunny tomorrow."

Section 2: Reporting Questions

EXERCISE 4

Report yes/no questions.

Use "if" or "whether": "Are you happy?" → He asked if I was happy.

Practice:

  • "Do you speak English?" → She asked if I spoke English.
  • "Have you been to Paris?" → He asked whether I had been to Paris.
  • "Will you come tomorrow?" → She asked if I would come the next day.
  • "Can you help me?" → He asked if I could help him.
EXERCISE 5

Report wh- questions.

"Where do you live?" → He asked where I lived. (No inversion!)

Practice:

  • "Where do you work?" → She asked where I worked.
  • "What time does it start?" → He asked what time it started.
  • "Why are you crying?" → She asked why I was crying.
  • "How long have you lived here?" → He asked how long I had lived there.
EXERCISE 6

Report questions someone asked you recently.

Model answer: "My boss asked me if I had finished the report. A stranger asked me where the station was. My friend asked me what I was doing at the weekend. My mother asked if I was eating well."

Section 3: Reporting Commands and Requests

EXERCISE 7

Report commands using "told + to".

"Close the door." → He told me to close the door.

Practice:

  • "Sit down." → The teacher told us to sit down.
  • "Don't be late." → She told me not to be late.
  • "Finish your homework." → My mother told me to finish my homework.
  • "Don't touch that." → He told me not to touch that.
EXERCISE 8

Report requests using "asked + to".

"Can you help me?" → She asked me to help her.

Practice:

  • "Please wait here." → He asked me to wait there.
  • "Could you open the window?" → She asked me to open the window.
  • "Would you mind helping me?" → He asked me to help him.

Section 4: Different Reporting Verbs

EXERCISE 9

Practice using different reporting verbs.

Verbs: say, tell, ask, explain, suggest, promise, warn, admit, deny, complain

Practice:

  • She promised she would help me.
  • He warned me not to go there alone.
  • She admitted she had made a mistake.
  • He denied taking the money.
  • She suggested going to a restaurant.
  • He complained that the service was terrible.
EXERCISE 10

Report these using appropriate verbs:

  • "I'll definitely be there." (promise)
  • "Be careful! It's dangerous." (warn)
  • "Yes, I ate your chocolate." (admit)
  • "Let's go to the beach." (suggest)
  • "No, I didn't break it." (deny)

Section 5: Changing Time and Place References

EXERCISE 11

Practice changing time expressions in reported speech.

today → that day, tomorrow → the next day, here → there, etc.

Practice:

  • "I'll see you tomorrow." → He said he would see me the next day.
  • "I saw him yesterday." → She said she had seen him the day before.
  • "I'm busy today." → He said he was busy that day.
  • "I'll do it now." → She said she would do it then.
  • "I live here." → He said he lived there.

Section 6: Reporting Conversations

EXERCISE 12

Report a conversation you had with a friend.

Model answer: "I was talking to Maria yesterday. She told me she had got a new job. I asked her where it was. She said it was in the city centre. She explained that she would start next month. I told her I was happy for her."

EXERCISE 13

Report what you heard about someone (gossip).

Model answer: "I heard that Juan and Ana had broken up. Someone told me that Juan had moved to another city. Ana said she was fine and that she was focusing on her career. Her friends said she seemed happier."

Section 7: Extended Monologue (2 minutes)

EXERCISE 14 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "An important conversation I had"

Describe a significant conversation using reported speech. Include:

  • Who you talked to
  • What they said
  • What they asked you
  • What you replied
EXERCISE 15 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "What people have told me about learning English"

Report advice and opinions you've received.

Structure: "My teacher told me that... She said... She advised me to... My friend suggested... Many people have said that..."

EXERCISE 16 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "Report a news story you read or heard"

Section 8: Role-Play Scenarios

EXERCISE 17 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Telling a friend about a job interview

Report what the interviewer asked and what you said.

EXERCISE 18 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Passing on a message

Someone called while your roommate was out. Tell them what the caller said.

Example: "Your mother called. She said she was coming tomorrow. She asked if you could pick her up. She told you to call her back."

EXERCISE 19 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Reporting a disagreement

Tell someone about an argument you witnessed.

Section 9: Quick Response Drills

QUICK DRILL

Transform to reported speech quickly:

  1. "I am happy." (She said...) → ?
  2. "I will come." (He said...) → ?
  3. "Do you like it?" (She asked...) → ?
  4. "Where is it?" (He asked...) → ?
  5. "Open the door." (She told me...) → ?
  6. "I have been there." (He said...) → ?
  7. "Don't worry." (She told me...) → ?
  8. "Can you help?" (He asked...) → ?

Answers:

  1. She said she was happy.
  2. He said he would come.
  3. She asked if I liked it.
  4. He asked where it was.
  5. She told me to open the door.
  6. He said he had been there.
  7. She told me not to worry.
  8. He asked if I could help.

Section 10: Spot and Correct the Error

ERROR CORRECTION

Say each sentence correctly:

  1. "She said me she was tired." → ?
  2. "He asked where was the station." → ?
  3. "She told that she would come." → ?
  4. "He asked did I like it." → ?
  5. "She said she will help." → ?
  6. "He told me don't worry." → ?
  7. "She asked me that I could help." → ?
  8. "He said he is busy today." (reporting next day) → ?

Correct answers:

  1. "She told me she was tired." / "She said she was tired."
  2. "He asked where the station was."
  3. "She said (that) she would come."
  4. "He asked if I liked it."
  5. "She said she would help."
  6. "He told me not to worry."
  7. "She asked me if I could help."
  8. "He said he was busy that day."
CONSEJO

Recuerda: SAY no lleva objeto directo. TELL sí lleva objeto: "She said..." vs "She told me...". En reported questions, no hay inversión: "He asked where it was" (NO "where was it").
