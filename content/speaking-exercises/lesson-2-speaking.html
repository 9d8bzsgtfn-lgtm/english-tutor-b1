Speaking Practice: Past Tenses

INSTRUCCIONES

Lee cada ejercicio en voz alta. Practica Past Simple y Past Continuous en contextos narrativos.

Objetivo: Contar historias y describir eventos pasados con fluidez.

Section 1: Describe Yesterday

EXERCISE 1

Describe what you did yesterday from morning to night.

Use Past Simple: I woke up, I had, I went, I worked...

Model answer: "Yesterday I woke up at 7. I had a quick breakfast and left home at 8. I worked all morning and had lunch at 1 pm. In the afternoon, I studied English. I watched TV in the evening and went to bed at 11."

EXERCISE 2

Talk about your last weekend.

Include: Saturday morning, Saturday afternoon, Saturday evening, Sunday.

Model answer: "Last Saturday morning, I slept late and had brunch at 11. In the afternoon, I went shopping with my friend. In the evening, we watched a film at the cinema. On Sunday, I visited my parents and we had lunch together."

EXERCISE 3

Describe your last holiday or trip.

Where did you go? What did you do? What did you see?

Model answer: "Last summer, I went to Italy. I stayed in Rome for a week. I visited the Colosseum and the Vatican. I ate delicious pasta every day. I took lots of photos. The weather was perfect."

Section 2: Past Continuous + Past Simple

EXERCISE 4

Tell a story using "while" or "when".

Background (Past Continuous) + Action (Past Simple). "While I was walking, I saw..."

Model answer: "I was walking to work when I saw an old friend. I was listening to music when my phone rang. While I was cooking dinner, the lights went out. While my parents were watching TV, I was studying in my room."

EXERCISE 5

Describe what different people were doing at a specific moment.

Example: "At 8 pm last night, my mother was cooking, my father was..."

Model answer: "At 8 pm last night, everyone in my family was doing something different. My mother was cooking dinner. My father was reading the newspaper. My sister was talking on the phone. I was studying for an exam. The dog was sleeping."

EXERCISE 6

Describe a scene from a film or book using past tenses.

Set the scene (Past Continuous) and describe actions (Past Simple).

Model answer: "It was raining heavily. The hero was walking alone through the dark streets. Suddenly, he heard a noise. He turned around and saw a shadow. Someone was following him. He started to run."

Section 3: Storytelling

EXERCISE 7

Tell a funny or embarrassing story from your life.

Use time connectors: First, Then, After that, Finally, Suddenly.

Structure: "This happened a few years ago. I was... when suddenly... First, I... Then... In the end..."

EXERCISE 8

Describe an important day in your life (graduation, wedding, birthday).

Model answer: "One of the most important days in my life was my graduation. I woke up feeling nervous. My whole family came to the ceremony. The sun was shining. When they called my name, I walked to the stage. Everyone was clapping. I felt so proud."

EXERCISE 9

Tell the story of how you met a good friend or partner.

Structure: "I met [name] in [year/place]. It was... I was doing... when... We started talking and... At first... Then..."

Section 4: Interrupted Actions

EXERCISE 10

Describe situations where one action interrupted another.

Use: was/were + -ing ... when ... Past Simple

Examples to practice:

  • "I was having a shower when the doorbell rang."
  • "They were playing football when it started to rain."
  • "She was crossing the street when she saw the accident."
  • "We were having dinner when the power went off."
EXERCISE 11

Create 5 sentences about interruptions in your own life.

Section 5: Asking Questions

EXERCISE 12

Practice asking questions in Past Simple.

Form: Did you...? / What did you...? / Where did you...?

Practice these questions:

  • "What did you do last weekend?"
  • "Where did you go on your last holiday?"
  • "Did you enjoy the film?"
  • "How did you get here?"
  • "Who did you see yesterday?"
  • "When did you start learning English?"
EXERCISE 13

Practice asking questions in Past Continuous.

Form: Were you...? / What were you doing...?

Practice these questions:

  • "What were you doing at 9 pm last night?"
  • "Were you sleeping when I called?"
  • "What were they talking about?"
  • "Was it raining when you left?"

Section 6: Extended Monologue (2 minutes)

EXERCISE 14 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "A memorable day from my childhood"

Speak for 2 minutes. Include:

  • How old you were
  • Where you were and what was happening
  • What happened (the main events)
  • How you felt
EXERCISE 15 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "The best/worst day of last year"

Describe in detail what made it the best or worst day.

EXERCISE 16 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "A news event I remember"

Describe what you were doing when you heard important news.

Structure: "I remember exactly what I was doing when... I was at... I was doing... Suddenly I heard/saw... I felt... Everyone around me was..."

Section 7: Role-Play Scenarios

EXERCISE 17 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Witness to an accident

A police officer asks you questions about an accident you saw. Answer:

  • "What were you doing when the accident happened?"
  • "What did you see?"
  • "What happened next?"
  • "Did you call for help?"
EXERCISE 18 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Telling a friend about your holiday

Your friend asks: "How was your holiday? Tell me everything!"

Section 8: Irregular Verbs Drill

VERB DRILL

Say the past tense quickly:

go → ? | see → ? | take → ? | make → ? | come → ? | give → ? | know → ? | think → ? | find → ? | tell → ? | buy → ? | bring → ? | write → ? | read → ? | speak → ? | eat → ? | drink → ? | sleep → ? | meet → ? | leave → ?

Answers: went, saw, took, made, came, gave, knew, thought, found, told, bought, brought, wrote, read, spoke, ate, drank, slept, met, left

Section 9: Quick Response Drills

QUICK DRILL

Answer these questions quickly:

  1. What did you have for breakfast today?
  2. Where did you go last weekend?
  3. What time did you wake up this morning?
  4. Did you watch TV last night?

Section 10: Spot and Correct the Error

ERROR CORRECTION

Say each sentence correctly:

  1. "I didn't went to the party." → ?
  2. "She was cook dinner when I arrived." → ?
  3. "Did you saw the match?" → ?
  4. "They were walking when they were seeing the accident." → ?
  5. "I readed that book last year." → ?
  6. "What you did yesterday?" → ?
