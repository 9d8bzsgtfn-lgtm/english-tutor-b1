Speaking Practice: Passive Voice

INSTRUCCIONES

Practica la voz pasiva para describir procesos, noticias y situaciones donde el agente no es importante.

Objetivo: Usar la voz pasiva de forma natural cuando el foco está en la acción, no en quién la realiza.

Section 1: Describing Processes

EXERCISE 1

Describe how chocolate is made.

Use: is/are + past participle. First... Then... After that... Finally...

Model answer: "First, cocoa beans are harvested. Then they are dried and roasted. The beans are ground into a paste. Sugar and milk are added. The mixture is heated and cooled. Finally, the chocolate is packaged and sold."

EXERCISE 2

Describe how wine is made.

Model answer: "Grapes are picked in autumn. They are crushed to extract the juice. The juice is put into barrels. It is left to ferment. The wine is aged for months or years. Finally, it is bottled and labeled."

EXERCISE 3

Describe a process you know (making coffee, sending an email, washing clothes).

Section 2: Reporting News and Facts

EXERCISE 4

Report these news items using passive voice.

The passive is common in news when we don't know/care who did it.

Model answers:

  • "A new hospital was opened yesterday."
  • "Three people were injured in the accident."
  • "The suspect has been arrested."
  • "The building was destroyed by fire."
  • "Several cars were stolen last night."
EXERCISE 5

Give historical facts using passive voice.

Model answers:

  • "America was discovered in 1492."
  • "The Eiffel Tower was built in 1889."
  • "The telephone was invented by Alexander Graham Bell."
  • "Romeo and Juliet was written by Shakespeare."
  • "The pyramids were constructed thousands of years ago."

Section 3: Rules and Regulations

EXERCISE 6

Describe rules using passive voice.

Rules are often expressed in passive: "Smoking is not allowed."

Model answer: "In this building, smoking is not allowed. Food and drinks are not permitted in the library. ID cards must be shown at the entrance. All visitors are required to sign in. Photographs may not be taken."

EXERCISE 7

Describe what is done in your workplace/school.

Model answer: "At my workplace, meetings are held every Monday. Emails are answered within 24 hours. Reports are submitted at the end of each month. Staff are trained regularly. Performance is reviewed annually."

Section 4: Passive with "By" (Agent)

EXERCISE 8

Practice passive sentences with "by" when the agent is important.

Model answers:

  • "This painting was created by Picasso."
  • "The Harry Potter books were written by J.K. Rowling."
  • "The company was founded by Steve Jobs."
  • "I was taught by an excellent teacher."
  • "The cake was made by my grandmother."
EXERCISE 9

Talk about things that were done by someone you know.

Section 5: Passive in Different Tenses

EXERCISE 10

Practice passive in present, past, and future.

Present: is made. Past: was made. Future: will be made.

Practice:

  • Present: "Coffee is grown in Brazil."
  • Past: "This house was built in 1920."
  • Present Perfect: "The work has been completed."
  • Future: "The meeting will be held tomorrow."
  • Modal: "This problem can be solved."

Section 6: Passive Questions

EXERCISE 12

Practice asking questions in passive voice.

Practice:

  • "When was this building constructed?"
  • "Where are these products made?"
  • "Has the report been finished?"
  • "Will the meeting be cancelled?"
  • "Was the window broken by accident?"
EXERCISE 13

Ask questions about famous things:

  • The Mona Lisa (Who / paint / by?)
  • The Great Wall of China (When / build?)
  • English (Where / speak?)

Section 7: Choosing Active or Passive

EXERCISE 14

Decide when to use passive and explain why.

Use passive when: 1) Agent unknown, 2) Agent not important, 3) Focusing on action/result

Compare:

  • "My bike was stolen." (agent unknown - passive better)
  • "My mother made this cake." (agent important - active better)
  • "Spanish is spoken in 20 countries." (general fact - passive better)
  • "The police arrested the thief." (agent known - active OK)

Section 8: Describing Your Town/Country

EXERCISE 15

Describe what is produced, grown, or made in your region.

Model answer: "In my region, olive oil is produced. Oranges are grown in the south. Cars are manufactured in the north. Wine is made in several areas. Many traditional crafts are still made by hand."

EXERCISE 16

Describe how your town/city has changed using passive.

Model answer: "Many new buildings have been built. The old market was demolished. A new park has been created. The streets have been modernized. Public transport has been improved."

Section 9: Extended Monologue (2 minutes)

EXERCISE 17 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "How a product you use every day is made"

Describe the process of making something (bread, paper, clothes, etc.).

EXERCISE 18 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "Famous inventions and discoveries"

Talk about when things were invented and by whom.

Structure: "The telephone was invented by... in... The first computer was built... Penicillin was discovered by... X-rays were discovered..."

EXERCISE 19 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "What needs to be done in my city/country"

Use passive with modals: should be done, must be changed, needs to be improved.

Section 11: Quick Response Drills

QUICK DRILL

Transform to passive quickly:

  1. They make wine in France. → Wine...
  2. Someone stole my wallet. → My wallet...
  3. They will open a new shop. → A new shop...
  4. Someone has broken the window. → The window...
  5. They are building a new road. → A new road...

Answers:

  1. "Wine is made in France."
  2. "My wallet was stolen."
  3. "A new shop will be opened."
  4. "The window has been broken."
  5. "A new road is being built."

Section 12: Spot and Correct the Error

  1. "The book was wrote by Shakespeare." → ?
  2. "The car was repair yesterday." → ?
  3. "English is spoke in many countries." → ?
  4. "The meeting has been cancel." → ?
  5. "This building built in 1990." → ?
  6. "The window was break by the ball." → ?

  8. "I was born in Madrid in 1990." → ? (correct or not?)

Correct answers:

  2. "The car was repaired yesterday."
  3. "English is spoken in many countries."

CONSEJO

Passive = be + past participle. Recuerda que el verbo "be" cambia según el tiempo: is/are, was/were, has been, will be. El past participle no cambia.
