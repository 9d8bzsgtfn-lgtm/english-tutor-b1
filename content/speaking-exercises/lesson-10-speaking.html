Speaking Practice: Relative Clauses

INSTRUCCIONES

Practica las oraciones de relativo para dar más información sobre personas, cosas y lugares.

Objetivo: Usar who, which, that, where, when para construir oraciones más complejas y fluidas.

Section 1: Defining Relatives - People (Who/That)

EXERCISE 1

Define people using "who" or "that".

A teacher is a person who teaches. / Someone that helps...

Model answers:

  • "A doctor is a person who treats sick people."
  • "A chef is someone who works in a kitchen."
  • "A vegetarian is a person who doesn't eat meat."
  • "A tourist is someone who visits places on holiday."
EXERCISE 2

Describe people you know using relative clauses.

Model answer: "I have a friend who lives in London. My brother is the person who helps me most. I work with people who are very friendly. The person who taught me English was excellent."

EXERCISE 3

Define these people:

  • A pilot
  • A lifeguard
  • An orphan
  • A witness

Section 2: Defining Relatives - Things (Which/That)

EXERCISE 4

Define objects using "which" or "that".

A dictionary is a book which/that explains words.

Model answers:

  • "A passport is a document which identifies you when travelling."
  • "A refrigerator is an appliance that keeps food cold."
  • "A smartphone is a device which does many things."
  • "An alarm clock is something that wakes you up."
EXERCISE 5

Describe things in your house using relative clauses.

Model answer: "I have a coffee machine which makes excellent coffee. The book that I'm reading is very interesting. The chair that I'm sitting on is very comfortable. I bought a lamp which gives good light."

EXERCISE 6

Define these objects:

  • A wallet
  • Sunglasses
  • An umbrella
  • A remote control

Section 3: Places (Where) and Times (When)

EXERCISE 7

Define places using "where".

A gym is a place where people exercise.

Model answers:

  • "A library is a place where you can borrow books."
  • "A hospital is a place where sick people are treated."
  • "A museum is a place where you can see art and history."
  • "A stadium is a place where sports events take place."
EXERCISE 8

Describe times using "when".

Christmas is a time when families get together.

Model answers:

  • "Summer is a season when many people go on holiday."
  • "Morning is a time when I feel most productive."
  • "My birthday is a day when I celebrate with friends."
  • "Winter is a period when it gets dark early."

Section 4: Object Relative Clauses (Can Omit)

EXERCISE 9

Practice sentences where you can omit the relative pronoun.

When the relative is the OBJECT: "The film (which) I saw..." - you can omit it.

Practice (with and without pronoun):

  • "The book (that) I'm reading is very good."
  • "The person (who) I met yesterday was friendly."
  • "The restaurant (which) we went to was excellent."
  • "The film (that) you recommended was boring."
EXERCISE 10

Talk about things you like, using object relative clauses.

Model answer: "The kind of music I like is pop. The food I enjoy most is Italian. The place I want to visit is Japan. The person I admire most is my grandmother."

Section 5: Non-Defining Relative Clauses

EXERCISE 11

Add extra information using non-defining clauses (with commas).

Cannot use "that" in non-defining. Must use commas when speaking (pause).

Practice:

  • "My sister, who lives in Paris, is a doctor."
  • "My car, which I bought last year, is very reliable."
  • "London, where I was born, is a fascinating city."
  • "January, when it's very cold, is my least favourite month."
EXERCISE 12

Add extra information about these:

  • Your best friend
  • Your hometown
  • Your favourite book or film
  • Your workplace or school

Section 6: Possession (Whose)

EXERCISE 13

Practice using "whose" to show possession.

A person whose... = de quien, cuyo/a

Practice:

  • "I have a friend whose father is a famous actor."
  • "That's the man whose car was stolen."
  • "She's the woman whose book I'm reading."
  • "People whose jobs are stressful need to relax."
EXERCISE 14

Describe people using "whose":

  • A person whose [job/hobby/family]...
  • Someone whose [idea/work/style]...

Section 7: Combining Sentences

EXERCISE 15

Combine these pairs of sentences using relative pronouns:

  1. I met a woman. She works at Google.
  2. I bought a phone. It has an amazing camera.
  3. This is the restaurant. We had dinner here last week.
  4. That's the day. We got married on that day.

Answers:

  1. "I met a woman who works at Google."
  2. "I bought a phone which has an amazing camera."
  3. "This is the restaurant where we had dinner last week."
  4. "That's the day when we got married."

Section 8: Extended Monologue (2 minutes)

EXERCISE 16 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "Describe your family and friends"

Use relative clauses to give more information about the people in your life.

Structure: "My mother, who is 60, works as a nurse. My best friend is someone who I've known since childhood. I have a cousin whose job involves a lot of travel..."

EXERCISE 17 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "Describe your neighbourhood"

Talk about places, buildings, and people in your area.

Structure: "I live in a neighbourhood which is very quiet. There's a park where children play. The bakery, which is on the corner, sells delicious bread. I know a man who has lived here for 50 years."

EXERCISE 18 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "Things that are important to me"

Describe objects, people, and places that matter to you using relative clauses.

Section 9: Role-Play Scenarios

EXERCISE 19 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Describing someone to help find them

Your friend is looking for someone at a party. Describe people:

  • "He's the man who is wearing a blue shirt."
  • "She's the woman whose hair is very long."
EXERCISE 20 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Estate agent showing a house

Describe the house and neighbourhood using relative clauses.

EXERCISE 21 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Giving directions

Use relative clauses: "Turn left at the building which has a red roof." "It's near the park where there's a fountain."

Section 10: Quick Response Drills

QUICK DRILL

Complete with the correct relative pronoun:

  1. A person ___ teaches is a teacher.
  2. That's the book ___ I'm reading.
  3. Madrid, ___ I was born, is beautiful.
  4. The day ___ I met you was special.
  5. People ___ children are small need patience.
  6. This is the restaurant ___ we ate.
  7. That's the woman ___ car was stolen.
  8. Something ___ helps you sleep is useful.

Answers:

  1. who/that
  2. which/that (or nothing - object)
  3. where
  4. when
  5. whose
  6. where
  7. whose
  8. which/that

Section 11: Spot and Correct the Error

ERROR CORRECTION

Say each sentence correctly:

  1. "The man which lives next door is nice." → ?
  2. "My car, that I bought last year, is red." → ?
  3. "The place where I met him there was nice." → ?
  4. "She's the woman who her son is famous." → ?
  5. "That's the reason why I left." → ? (correct or not?)
  6. "I met a man his wife works with me." → ?
  7. "London, that is the capital, is big." → ?
  8. "The book who I read was good." → ?

Correct answers:

  1. "The man who lives next door is nice."
  2. "My car, which I bought last year, is red."
  3. "The place where I met him was nice."
  4. "She's the woman whose son is famous."
  5. CORRECT!
  6. "I met a man whose wife works with me."
  7. "London, which is the capital, is big."
  8. "The book which I read was good."
CONSEJO

WHO = personas, WHICH = cosas, WHERE = lugares, WHEN = tiempos, WHOSE = posesión. En non-defining (con comas), NUNCA uses "that".
