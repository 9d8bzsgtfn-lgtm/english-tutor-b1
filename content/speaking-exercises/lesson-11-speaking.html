Speaking Practice: Phrasal Verbs

INSTRUCCIONES

Practica los phrasal verbs más comunes para hablar de forma más natural y coloquial.

Objetivo: Usar phrasal verbs con confianza en conversaciones cotidianas.

Section 1: Daily Routine Phrasal Verbs

EXERCISE 1

Describe your morning using phrasal verbs.

Use: wake up, get up, turn on/off, put on, take off

Model answer: "I wake up at 7. I get up immediately and turn on the radio. I take off my pyjamas and put on my clothes. I turn off the lights when I leave. I usually set off for work at 8."

EXERCISE 2

Describe your evening routine.

Use: come back, sit down, take off, put away, turn off, go to bed

Model answer: "I come back home around 6. I take off my shoes and sit down on the sofa. I put away my things and turn on the TV. Later, I turn off all the lights and go to bed."

Section 2: Social Life Phrasal Verbs

EXERCISE 3

Talk about social activities using phrasal verbs.

Use: get together, hang out, go out, meet up, catch up, get along

Model answer: "I often hang out with my friends on weekends. We usually meet up at a café. Sometimes we go out for dinner. I get along well with most people. When I see old friends, we catch up on each other's news."

EXERCISE 4

Describe a party or social event.

Use: turn up, show up, dress up, pick up, drop off

Model answer: "Last Saturday, I went to a party. I dressed up nicely. My friend picked me up at 8. Lots of people turned up. Everyone was having fun. My friend dropped me off at midnight."

Section 3: Work and Study Phrasal Verbs

EXERCISE 5

Talk about work using phrasal verbs.

Use: carry on, deal with, put off, take on, hand in, fill in

Model answer: "At work, I have to deal with many problems. I try not to put off difficult tasks. I've taken on a new project. I need to fill in some forms and hand them in by Friday. I'll carry on working until it's done."

EXERCISE 6

Talk about learning English using phrasal verbs.

Use: look up, pick up, keep up, give up, go over, figure out

Model answer: "When I don't know a word, I look it up in a dictionary. I've picked up a lot of vocabulary from films. I try to keep up with my studies and never give up. I go over my notes regularly. Sometimes grammar rules are hard to figure out."

Section 4: Technology Phrasal Verbs

EXERCISE 7

Talk about using technology.

Use: turn on/off, log in/out, sign up, set up, back up, look up

Model answer: "Every morning, I turn on my computer and log in. I signed up for several websites. I need to set up a new account. I always back up my files. I often look up information online."

EXERCISE 8

Describe a technical problem.

Use: break down, shut down, switch off, plug in, sort out

Model answer: "My computer broke down last week. It just shut down suddenly. I tried switching it off and on again. I made sure it was plugged in. Eventually, a technician helped me sort out the problem."

Section 5: Separable Phrasal Verbs Practice

EXERCISE 9

Practice separable phrasal verbs (the object can go in the middle).

turn off the TV / turn the TV off / turn it off (NOT "turn off it")

Practice with pronouns:

  • "I need to put away my clothes." → "I need to put them away."
  • "Can you turn down the music?" → "Can you turn it down?"
  • "I'll pick up my sister." → "I'll pick her up."
  • "Please fill in this form." → "Please fill it in."
EXERCISE 10

Replace the noun with a pronoun in these sentences:

  1. I turned off the computer.
  2. She put on her jacket.
  3. He threw away the old papers.
  4. They picked up the children.

Section 6: Inseparable Phrasal Verbs

EXERCISE 11

Practice inseparable phrasal verbs (object must come after).

look after, look for, get on, get off, run into, come across

Practice:

  • "I'm looking for my keys." (NOT "looking my keys for")
  • "She looks after her grandmother."
  • "I ran into an old friend yesterday."
  • "We got on the bus at 8."
  • "I came across an interesting article."

Section 7: Common Combinations

EXERCISE 12

Practice these common phrasal verb families:

GET family:

  • get up (levantarse)
  • get on (with) (llevarse bien)
  • get off (bajarse)
  • get over (superar)
  • get back (volver)

TAKE family:

  • take off (quitarse / despegar)
  • take on (asumir)
  • take up (empezar hobby)
  • take after (parecerse)
  • take care of (cuidar)
EXERCISE 13

Create sentences using these phrasal verb families:

LOOK: look for, look after, look up, look forward to

PUT: put on, put off, put away, put up with

Section 8: Storytelling with Phrasal Verbs

EXERCISE 14

Tell a story about a trip using phrasal verbs.

set off, check in, take off, get on, pick up, drop off, turn up

Model answer: "Last month, I went on holiday. I set off early in the morning. I checked in at the airport and the plane took off on time. When I arrived, I picked up my luggage. A taxi dropped me off at the hotel. Everything turned out great!"

EXERCISE 15

Tell a story about starting a new hobby.

take up, sign up, find out, carry on, give up, keep up

Section 9: Extended Monologue (2 minutes)

EXERCISE 16 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "A typical day in my life" using at least 10 phrasal verbs

Structure: "I wake up at 7 and get up quickly. I turn on the radio and put on my clothes. I set off for work at 8. At work, I deal with emails and carry on with my projects. I get back home at 6. I take off my shoes and sit down..."

EXERCISE 17 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "How I stay healthy"

Use: work out, cut down on, give up, take up, keep fit, put on weight, look after

EXERCISE 18 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "Challenges I have overcome"

Use: deal with, get through, get over, sort out, figure out, carry on

Section 11: Quick Response Drills

QUICK DRILL

Give the meaning in Spanish quickly:

wake up → ? | give up → ? | look for → ? | turn off → ? | carry on → ? | put off → ? | get on with → ? | look after → ? | find out → ? | run out of → ?

Answers: despertarse, rendirse, buscar, apagar, continuar, posponer, llevarse bien con, cuidar, descubrir, quedarse sin

QUICK DRILL

Replace with a phrasal verb:

  1. extinguish (fire) → ?
  2. continue → ?
  3. postpone → ?
  4. tolerate → ?
  5. investigate → ?
  6. resemble → ?

Answers: put out, carry on / go on, put off, put up with, look into, take after

Section 12: Spot and Correct the Error

ERROR CORRECTION

Say each sentence correctly:

  1. "I turned off it." → ?
  2. "I'm looking for it my keys." → ?
  3. "She put her coat on." → ? (correct or not?)
  4. "I ran into to an old friend." → ?
  5. "He gave the smoking up." → ?
  6. "They get well on." → ?
  7. "I look forward to see you." → ?
  8. "Please sit down yourself." → ?

Correct answers:

  1. "I turned it off."
  2. "I'm looking for my keys."
  3. CORRECT! (Both "put on her coat" and "put her coat on" work)
  4. "I ran into an old friend."
  5. "He gave up smoking."
  6. "They get on well."
  7. "I look forward to seeing you."
  8. "Please sit down."
CONSEJO

Los phrasal verbs son esenciales para sonar natural en inglés. Apréndelos en contexto, no como listas aisladas. Cuando uses pronombres con phrasal verbs separables, el pronombre DEBE ir en medio.
