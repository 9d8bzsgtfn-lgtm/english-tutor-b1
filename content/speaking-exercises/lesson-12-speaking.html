Section 1: All Tenses Review

EXERCISE 1

Talk about yourself using all the tenses you've learned.

Include: Present Simple, Present Continuous, Past Simple, Past Continuous, Present Perfect, Future

Model answer: "I'm a student. I study English. Right now, I'm preparing for an exam. Yesterday, I reviewed all my notes. While I was studying, my friend called. I've learned a lot this year. I've been working hard for months. Tomorrow, I'm going to practice more. I think I'll pass the exam."

EXERCISE 2

Tell a story that uses multiple tenses.

Topic: "How I started learning English"

Structure:

  • Background (Past): "I started learning English when I was 10..."
  • Progress (Present Perfect): "Since then, I've improved a lot..."
  • Current situation (Present): "Now I study every day..."
  • Future plans (Future): "I'm going to take an exam..."

Section 2: Comparing and Contrasting

EXERCISE 3

Compare two photos (typical exam task).

Imagine Photo A: People at a busy office. Photo B: Person working from home.

Model answer: "In the first photo, I can see people working in an office. They're sitting at desks and talking to colleagues. In the second photo, there's a person working from home. They look more relaxed. The office is noisier than working from home. Working from home is more flexible, but the office is better for teamwork. I think both have advantages and disadvantages."

EXERCISE 4

Compare two options and give your opinion.

Topic: Living in a city vs living in the countryside

Section 3: Expressing and Justifying Opinions

EXERCISE 5

Express your opinion on these topics:

  1. Social media: good or bad for society?
  2. Should students wear uniforms?
  3. Is technology making us lazy?

Use: I think..., In my opinion..., I believe..., I agree/disagree because...

Structure: "In my opinion, [topic] is... because... On the one hand... On the other hand... Overall, I believe..."

EXERCISE 6

Agree or disagree with these statements:

  1. "Money can buy happiness."
  2. "Everyone should learn a foreign language."
  3. "Online learning is as effective as classroom learning."

Section 4: Hypothetical Situations

EXERCISE 7

Answer these hypothetical questions:

  1. What would you do if you had more free time?
  2. If you could change one thing about your country, what would it be?
  3. What would happen if there were no internet?
EXERCISE 8

Give advice using "If I were you..."

Situations:

  1. A friend wants to improve their English quickly.
  2. Someone is stressed about exams.
  3. A colleague is having problems with their boss.

Section 5: Describing Experiences

EXERCISE 9

Describe a memorable experience from your life.

Topics to choose from:

  • The best holiday you've ever had
  • A challenging situation you overcame
  • An embarrassing moment
  • Meeting someone who changed your life
EXERCISE 10

Describe changes in your life or your country.

Use Present Perfect: "has changed", "has become", "has improved"

Section 6: Explaining and Describing

EXERCISE 11

Explain a process without naming it (like Taboo).

Examples:

  • Explain how to make coffee
  • Explain how to book a flight online
  • Explain how to change a car tyre
EXERCISE 12

Describe these without using the word:

  1. A smartphone (without saying "phone")
  2. A teacher (without saying "teach")
  3. A hospital (without saying "doctor" or "sick")

Use relative clauses: "It's something which...", "It's a person who..."

Section 7: Exam-Style Monologues (2 minutes each)

EXERCISE 13 - EXAM MONOLOGUE

Topic: "My hometown"

Include:

  • Where it is and basic description
  • What you like about it
  • What you don't like or would change
  • How it has changed over the years
EXERCISE 14 - EXAM MONOLOGUE

Topic: "Technology in daily life"

Include:

  • How you use technology every day
  • Advantages and disadvantages
  • How technology has changed our lives
  • Your opinion about future technology
EXERCISE 15 - EXAM MONOLOGUE

Topic: "Health and fitness"

Include:

  • What you do to stay healthy
  • Exercise and diet habits
  • Advice for others (should, must, have to)
  • Changes you've made or want to make
EXERCISE 16 - EXAM MONOLOGUE

Topic: "My ideal job"

Include:

  • What job you would like and why
  • What skills you would need
  • Comparison with your current situation

Section 8: Interactive Role-Plays

EXERCISE 17 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Planning an event with a friend

Decide together:

  • What type of event (party, trip, dinner)
  • When and where
  • Who to invite
  • What to prepare

Use: suggestions (shall we, how about), conditionals (if we..., we could...)

EXERCISE 18 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Complaining at a restaurant

Your food is cold, the service is slow. Be polite but firm.

Use: Could you..., I'm afraid..., I'd like to...

EXERCISE 19 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Doctor's appointment

Describe symptoms, answer questions, receive advice.

Section 10: Fluency and Filler Phrases

EXERCISE 21

Practice using filler phrases to sound more natural.

Useful phrases:

  • Thinking: "Well...", "Let me think...", "That's a good question..."
  • Opinions: "In my opinion...", "I think that...", "Personally..."
  • Examples: "For example...", "For instance...", "Such as..."
  • Connecting: "However...", "On the other hand...", "Also..."
  • Concluding: "So overall...", "To sum up...", "In conclusion..."

Section 11: Common Mistakes Review

ERROR CORRECTION REVIEW

Correct these common B1 mistakes:

  1. "I am agree with you."
  2. "I'm living here since 2020."
  3. "She suggested me to go."
  4. "I went to London last year. I have visited Big Ben."
  5. "If I will have time, I'll help you."
  6. "The people is friendly."
  7. "I must to study more."
  8. "She told me that she will come."
  9. "This is the book who I read."
  10. "I'm looking forward to see you."

Correct answers:

  1. "I agree with you."
  2. "I've been living here since 2020." / "I've lived here since 2020."
  3. "She suggested going." / "She suggested that I go."
  4. "I went to London last year. I visited Big Ben."
  5. "If I have time, I'll help you."
  6. "The people are friendly." / "People are friendly."
  7. "I must study more."
  8. "She told me that she would come."
  9. "This is the book which I read."
  10. "I'm looking forward to seeing you."

Section 12: Self-Assessment Checklist

SELF-ASSESSMENT

Check your B1 speaking skills. Can you...?

  • Talk about daily routines and habits?
  • Tell stories about the past?
  • Talk about experiences using Present Perfect?
  • Discuss future plans and predictions?
  • Compare things and people?
  • Give advice using modal verbs?
  • Talk about hypothetical situations?
  • Describe processes using passive voice?
  • Report what people said?
  • Add information using relative clauses?
  • Use phrasal verbs naturally?
  • Express opinions and justify them?
EXAM TIPS
  • Don't panic if you make mistakes - correct yourself and continue.
  • Use a variety of structures to show your range.
  • Give full answers, not just "yes" or "no".
  • Use linking words to connect your ideas.
  • Practice speaking about familiar topics for 2 minutes.
  • Listen carefully to questions before answering.
