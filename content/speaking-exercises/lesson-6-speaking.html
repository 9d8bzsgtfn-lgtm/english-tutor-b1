Speaking Practice: Modal Verbs

INSTRUCCIONES

Practica los verbos modales para expresar habilidad, permiso, obligación y posibilidad.

Objetivo: Usar los modales correctos según el contexto comunicativo.

Section 1: Ability (Can, Could, Be able to)

EXERCISE 1

Talk about things you can do now.

Use: I can + verb (present ability)

Model answer: "I can speak two languages. I can drive a car. I can cook basic meals. I can play the guitar. I can't swim very well. I can't sing at all."

EXERCISE 2

Talk about things you could do when you were a child.

Use: I could + verb (past ability)

Model answer: "When I was a child, I could run very fast. I could climb trees easily. I couldn't read until I was 6. I could stay awake all night. I couldn't cook anything."

EXERCISE 3

Talk about abilities you want to develop.

Use: I want to be able to..., I hope I'll be able to...

Model answer: "I want to be able to speak English fluently. I hope I'll be able to travel more. I'd like to be able to play the piano. I want to be able to cook gourmet meals."

Section 2: Permission (Can, Could, May)

EXERCISE 4

Practice asking for permission politely.

Can = informal, Could = polite, May = formal

Practice:

  • "Can I borrow your pen?" (informal)
  • "Could I use your phone?" (polite)
  • "May I ask you a question?" (formal)
  • "Could I possibly leave early today?" (very polite)
EXERCISE 5

Talk about rules - what you can and can't do in different places.

Model answer: "In a library, you can't talk loudly. You can borrow books. You can use the computers. You can't eat or drink. At work, we can take a lunch break. We can't use personal phones during meetings."

Section 3: Obligation (Must, Have to, Should)

EXERCISE 6

Talk about things you must/have to do.

Must = personal/strong, Have to = external rule

Model answer: "I have to go to work every day. I must finish this project by Friday. I have to pay my bills on time. I must remember to call my mother. We have to wear a uniform at work."

EXERCISE 7

Talk about things you don't have to do (no obligation).

Use: don't have to (it's not necessary)

Model answer: "I don't have to work on weekends. I don't have to wear a suit to work. You don't have to pay now - you can pay later. We don't have to bring food - they'll provide it."

EXERCISE 8

Talk about things you mustn't do (prohibition).

Use: mustn't (it's forbidden, don't do it!)

Model answer: "You mustn't smoke in the hospital. You mustn't drive without a licence. We mustn't be late for the meeting. You mustn't tell anyone - it's a secret."

Section 4: Advice (Should, Shouldn't, Ought to)

EXERCISE 9

Give advice about healthy living.

Use: You should / You shouldn't / You ought to

Model answer: "You should exercise regularly. You shouldn't eat too much sugar. You should drink plenty of water. You shouldn't smoke. You ought to get enough sleep. You should reduce stress."

EXERCISE 10

Give advice to someone learning English.

Model answer: "You should practice every day. You shouldn't be afraid of making mistakes. You should watch films in English. You ought to read books in English. You shouldn't only study grammar - speaking is important too."

EXERCISE 11

Give advice to someone visiting your city/country.

Section 5: Possibility (May, Might, Could)

EXERCISE 12

Talk about possibilities for the weekend.

Use: might / may / could (maybe, perhaps)

Model answer: "This weekend, I might go to the cinema. I may visit my parents. It could rain, so I might stay home. I might not go out if I'm tired. We could have a barbecue if the weather is nice."

EXERCISE 13

Speculate about a situation.

Someone is not answering their phone. Why?

Model answer: "They might be busy. They could be in a meeting. They may have left their phone at home. It might be on silent. They could be driving. They may not have seen my call."

Section 6: Requests and Offers

EXERCISE 14

Practice making polite requests.

Can you...? Could you...? Would you mind...?

Practice:

  • "Can you help me with this?"
  • "Could you open the window, please?"
  • "Would you mind waiting a moment?"
  • "Could you possibly lend me some money?"
EXERCISE 15

Practice making offers.

Can I...? Shall I...? Would you like me to...?

Practice:

  • "Can I help you with those bags?"
  • "Shall I make you a coffee?"
  • "Would you like me to explain it again?"
  • "Can I get you anything?"

Section 7: Extended Monologue (2 minutes)

EXERCISE 16 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "Rules and regulations at my workplace/school"

Speak for 2 minutes about:

  • What you have to do
  • What you mustn't do
  • What you can do
  • What you should do to be successful
EXERCISE 17 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "Skills I have and skills I want to develop"

Talk about what you can do, what you could do before, what you want to be able to do.

EXERCISE 18 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "Advice for tourists visiting my country"

Use should/shouldn't, must/mustn't, can/can't.

Section 8: Role-Play Scenarios

EXERCISE 19 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Doctor and patient

The doctor gives advice:

  • "You should rest more."
  • "You mustn't smoke."
  • "You have to take this medicine twice a day."
EXERCISE 20 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Asking your boss for permission

  • "Could I leave early today?"
  • "Would it be possible to work from home?"
  • "May I take next Friday off?"
EXERCISE 21 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Explaining rules to a new employee

Use: You have to..., You mustn't..., You can..., You should...

Section 9: Quick Response Drills

QUICK DRILL

Choose the correct modal and say the sentence:

  1. Ask permission politely to open window → ?
  2. It's not necessary to pay now → ?
  3. It's forbidden to smoke here → ?
  4. Give advice: sleep more → ?
  5. Maybe it will rain → ?
  6. You have the ability to drive → ?
  7. Past ability: speak French as a child → ?
  8. Make an offer: help carry bags → ?

Answers:

  1. "Could I open the window?"
  2. "You don't have to pay now."
  3. "You mustn't smoke here."
  4. "You should sleep more."
  5. "It might rain."
  6. "I can drive."
  7. "I could speak French as a child."
  8. "Can I help you carry those bags?"

Section 10: Spot and Correct the Error

ERROR CORRECTION

Say each sentence correctly:

  1. "I can to swim." → ?
  2. "You must to go now." → ?
  3. "She cans speak French." → ?
  4. "You don't must smoke here." → ?
  5. "I must not to forget." → ?
  6. "Could you to help me?" → ?
  7. "He should goes to the doctor." → ?
  8. "I don't can understand." → ?

Correct answers:

  1. "I can swim."
  2. "You must go now."
  3. "She can speak French."
  4. "You mustn't smoke here."
  5. "I mustn't forget."
  6. "Could you help me?"
  7. "He should go to the doctor."
  8. "I can't understand."
CONSEJO

Los modales NO llevan "to" después (excepto "have to", "ought to"). El verbo después del modal siempre va en infinitivo sin "to".
