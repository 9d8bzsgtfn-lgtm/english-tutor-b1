Speaking Practice: Present Tenses

INSTRUCCIONES

Lee cada ejercicio en voz alta. Graba tu respuesta usando el micrófono o practica con el tutor en el chat.

Objetivo: Practicar Present Simple y Present Continuous en contextos reales.

Section 1: Describe Your Daily Routine

EXERCISE 1

Describe your typical morning routine.

Use Present Simple. Include: wake up, get up, have breakfast, leave home.

Model answer: "I usually wake up at 7 o'clock. I get up immediately and take a shower. Then I have breakfast - I always have coffee and toast. I leave home at 8:30."

EXERCISE 2

What do you do on weekends?

Use Present Simple with frequency adverbs: always, usually, sometimes, never.

Model answer: "On weekends, I usually sleep late. I sometimes meet friends for lunch. I always spend time with my family on Sundays. I never work on Saturdays."

EXERCISE 3

Describe what your family members do during a typical day.

Use third person (he/she/they). Remember: he works, she studies.

Model answer: "My father works in an office. He starts at 9 and finishes at 6. My mother teaches at a school. She gets up very early. My brother studies at university."

Section 2: Describe What's Happening Now

EXERCISE 4

Look around you. What is happening right now?

Use Present Continuous. Example: "The sun is shining."

Model answer: "Right now, I'm sitting at my desk. I'm studying English. The birds are singing outside. My phone is charging on the table."

EXERCISE 5

Imagine you're in a café. Describe what people are doing.

Use: A man is..., Two women are..., The waiter is...

Model answer: "A man is reading a newspaper. Two women are having coffee and talking. The waiter is taking an order. A child is eating a cake. Some people are working on their laptops."

EXERCISE 6

Describe a busy street scene.

Use Present Continuous for actions in progress.

Model answer: "Many people are walking on the street. Cars are moving slowly. A bus is stopping at the bus stop. Some tourists are taking photos. A street musician is playing guitar."

Section 3: Contrast Present Simple vs Continuous

EXERCISE 7

Compare what you usually do vs what you're doing today.

Example: "I usually work from home, but today I'm working at the office."

Model answer: "I usually drink coffee, but today I'm drinking tea. I normally study in the morning, but today I'm studying in the afternoon. I always walk to work, but today I'm taking the bus."

EXERCISE 8

Talk about temporary vs permanent situations.

Permanent = Present Simple. Temporary = Present Continuous.

Model answer: "I live in Madrid - that's my permanent home. But this month I'm staying with my parents in Barcelona. I work as a teacher, but this week I'm helping in the office."

Section 4: Stative Verbs Practice

EXERCISE 9

Express your opinions and feelings about things.

Use stative verbs: like, love, hate, prefer, want, need, believe, think.

Model answer: "I love learning English. I like listening to podcasts. I prefer studying in the morning. I believe practice is important. I think grammar is useful."

EXERCISE 10

Describe what you know, understand, and remember about a topic.

Use: know, understand, remember, forget, realize.

Model answer: "I know the difference between Present Simple and Present Continuous. I understand when to use each tense. I remember that stative verbs don't use -ing. I sometimes forget the irregular forms."

Section 5: Asking Questions

EXERCISE 11

Ask questions about daily routines (Present Simple).

Form: Do you...? / Does he...? / What time do you...?

Model questions: "What time do you wake up? Do you have breakfast every day? Does your sister work? Where do your parents live? How often do you exercise?"

EXERCISE 12

Ask questions about what's happening now (Present Continuous).

Form: Are you...? / Is she...? / What are you...?

Model questions: "What are you doing right now? Are you studying? Is it raining outside? Who are you talking to? Why are they running?"

Section 6: Extended Monologue (2 minutes)

EXERCISE 13 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "My typical week"

Speak for 2 minutes about your typical week. Include:

  • Your work/study routine
  • Your free time activities
  • What you're doing this week that's different
  • Your likes and preferences

Structure guide:

"During the week, I usually... On weekdays, I... In the evenings, I often..."

"At weekends, I normally... I always... I never..."

"This week is different because I'm..."

"I really like... I prefer... I don't enjoy..."

EXERCISE 14 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "A day in the life of someone I admire"

Describe a typical day for a person you admire (family member, celebrity, historical figure).

Structure guide:

"I'm going to talk about [name]. He/She is a..."

"Every day, he/she wakes up... He/She works as..."

"In the evening, he/she usually..."

"I admire him/her because..."

Section 7: Role-Play Scenarios

EXERCISE 15 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Phone call to a friend

Your friend calls you. Answer these questions naturally:

  • "Hi! What are you doing?"
  • "Do you want to come to the cinema tonight?"
  • "What time do you usually finish work?"
EXERCISE 16 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Job interview

Answer these interview questions:

  • "Tell me about your daily routine."
  • "What are you studying at the moment?"
  • "How do you spend your free time?"

Section 8: Picture Description Practice

EXERCISE 17

Imagine a picture of a park on a sunny day. Describe it.

Use Present Continuous for what people are doing.

Model answer: "In this picture, I can see a park. The sun is shining. Some children are playing on the swings. A couple is having a picnic. An old man is feeding the pigeons. A woman is jogging along the path. Dogs are running on the grass."

EXERCISE 18

Imagine a picture of an office. Describe the scene.

Model answer: "This is a modern office. Several people are working at their computers. A man is talking on the phone. Two colleagues are having a meeting in the corner. Someone is drinking coffee. The boss is giving a presentation."

Section 10: Spot and Correct the Error

ERROR CORRECTION

Say each sentence correctly:

  1. "I am living in Madrid." (permanent situation) → ?
  2. "She work in a hospital." → ?
  3. "They are wanting a new car." → ?
  4. "What you are doing?" → ?
  5. "He don't like pizza." → ?
  6. "I'm knowing the answer." → ?
  7. "Does she works here?" → ?
  8. "Right now I study English." → ?

Correct answers:

  1. "I live in Madrid."
  2. "She works in a hospital."
  3. "They want a new car."
  4. "What are you doing?"
  5. "He doesn't like pizza."
  6. "I know the answer."
  7. "Does she work here?"
  8. "Right now I'm studying English."
