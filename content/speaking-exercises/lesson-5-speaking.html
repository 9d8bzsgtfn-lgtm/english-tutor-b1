Speaking Practice: Comparatives & Superlatives

INSTRUCCIONES

Practica comparando personas, lugares y cosas usando adjetivos comparativos y superlativos.

Objetivo: Hacer comparaciones con fluidez y precisión.

Section 1: Comparing Two Things

EXERCISE 1

Compare two cities you know.

Use: bigger than, more expensive than, older than, better than...

Model answer: "Madrid is bigger than my hometown. It's more crowded and noisier. But my hometown is quieter and cleaner. Madrid is more exciting, but my hometown is safer. Life is cheaper in my hometown."

EXERCISE 2

Compare two people in your family.

Compare: height, age, personality, interests.

Model answer: "My brother is taller than me. He's also older. But I'm more patient than him. He's funnier than me. I'm more organized than he is. We're both good at sports, but he's better at football."

EXERCISE 3

Compare two hobbies or sports.

Model answer: "Swimming is more relaxing than running. But running is cheaper because you don't need a pool. Running is harder for me. Swimming is better for your joints. I think swimming is more enjoyable."

Section 2: The Best, Worst, Most...

EXERCISE 4

Talk about "the best" and "the worst" in your life.

The best... I've ever..., The worst... in my life...

Model answer: "The best film I've ever seen is The Godfather. The worst meal I've ever had was at a terrible restaurant in town. The best holiday I've had was in Italy. The happiest day of my life was my graduation."

EXERCISE 5

Describe the "mosts" in your country/city.

The most famous, the oldest, the biggest, the most beautiful...

Model answer: "The most famous monument in Spain is the Sagrada Familia. The biggest city is Madrid. The oldest university is Salamanca. The most popular sport is football. The most visited region is Catalonia."

EXERCISE 6

Talk about people who are "the most" in your life.

Model answer: "My grandmother is the wisest person I know. My best friend is the funniest. My mother is the most patient person in my family. My boss is the most hardworking. My cousin is the most adventurous."

Section 3: Equality (As...As)

EXERCISE 7

Compare things that are similar using "as...as".

Use: as tall as, as expensive as, as good as, not as...as

Model answer: "My brother is as tall as me. This restaurant is as good as the one we went to last week. This book isn't as interesting as I expected. The exam wasn't as difficult as I thought."

EXERCISE 8

Use common expressions with "as...as".

As cold as ice, as quiet as a mouse, as easy as pie...

Practice:

  • "The water was as cold as ice."
  • "She was as quiet as a mouse."
  • "This exercise is as easy as pie."
  • "He's as strong as an ox."
  • "I slept like a log - as peaceful as a baby."

Section 4: Less and More

EXERCISE 9

Use "less" and "more" to compare.

Less expensive = cheaper, more difficult = harder

Practice:

  • "This option is less expensive but more practical."
  • "Working from home is less stressful but sometimes more isolating."
  • "Small cars use less fuel and are more economical."
  • "I have more time now but less money."

Section 5: Changes Over Time

EXERCISE 10

Describe how things have changed using comparatives.

Use: getting + comparative, more and more, less and less

Model answer: "Technology is getting cheaper and more advanced. Life is getting more expensive. People are becoming more health-conscious. The weather is getting warmer. Cities are becoming more crowded."

EXERCISE 11

Describe how YOU have changed over the years.

Model answer: "I'm more confident than I was 5 years ago. I'm less nervous about public speaking. I've become more patient. I'm getting better at managing stress. I'm less impulsive than I used to be."

Section 6: Making Recommendations

EXERCISE 12

Recommend things using comparatives and superlatives.

Model answer: "If you want the best coffee in town, go to Café Central. It's better than all the other cafés. The food is also the most authentic. It's a bit more expensive, but it's definitely the best value for money."

EXERCISE 13

Compare products and give your recommendation.

Compare two phones, cars, restaurants, or shops.

Section 7: Extended Monologue (2 minutes)

EXERCISE 14 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "Compare your country with another country"

Speak for 2 minutes comparing:

  • Size and population
  • Weather and climate
  • Cost of living
  • Culture and lifestyle
EXERCISE 15 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "Life now vs life 50 years ago"

Compare technology, communication, travel, work, health.

Structure: "Life today is very different from life 50 years ago. Technology is much more advanced. Communication is faster and easier. Travel is cheaper and more accessible. However, life was perhaps simpler and less stressful."

EXERCISE 16 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "The best places I've visited"

Describe and compare different places you've been to.

Section 8: Role-Play Scenarios

EXERCISE 17 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Shopping for a new phone

Compare different phones with a shop assistant:

  • "Which one has a better camera?"
  • "Is this one cheaper than that one?"
  • "Which is the best value for money?"
EXERCISE 18 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Choosing a holiday destination

Discuss with a friend: beach vs. mountains, city vs. countryside.

EXERCISE 19 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Job interview - comparing your skills

Explain why you're better than other candidates.

Section 9: Quick Response Drills

QUICK DRILL - COMPARATIVES

Give the comparative quickly:

big → ? | good → ? | bad → ? | far → ? | expensive → ? | happy → ? | interesting → ? | hot → ? | little → ? | beautiful → ?

Answers: bigger, better, worse, farther/further, more expensive, happier, more interesting, hotter, less, more beautiful

QUICK DRILL - SUPERLATIVES

Give the superlative quickly:

big → ? | good → ? | bad → ? | far → ? | expensive → ? | happy → ? | interesting → ? | hot → ? | little → ? | beautiful → ?

Answers: the biggest, the best, the worst, the farthest/furthest, the most expensive, the happiest, the most interesting, the hottest, the least, the most beautiful

Section 10: Spot and Correct the Error

ERROR CORRECTION

Say each sentence correctly:

  1. "She is more taller than me." → ?
  2. "This is the most best film." → ?
  3. "He is more intelligent that his brother." → ?
  4. "This book is interestinger than that one." → ?
  5. "It's not as expensive than I thought." → ?
  6. "He's the most old person I know." → ?
  7. "My house is biger than yours." → ?
  8. "This is the worse decision ever." → ?

Correct answers:

  1. "She is taller than me."
  2. "This is the best film."
  3. "He is more intelligent than his brother."
  4. "This book is more interesting than that one."
  5. "It's not as expensive as I thought."
  6. "He's the oldest person I know."
  7. "My house is bigger than yours."
  8. "This is the worst decision ever."
CONSEJO

Recuerda: adjetivos cortos (1 sílaba) = -er/-est. Adjetivos largos (2+ sílabas) = more/most. Excepciones: good-better-best, bad-worse-worst.
