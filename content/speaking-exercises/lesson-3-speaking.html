Speaking Practice: Present Perfect

INSTRUCCIONES

Practica Present Perfect Simple y Continuous para hablar de experiencias y acciones conectadas con el presente.

Objetivo: Usar el Present Perfect con naturalidad para hablar de experiencias y resultados.

Section 1: Life Experiences

EXERCISE 1

Talk about things you have done in your life.

Use: I have + past participle. I've visited, I've tried, I've seen...

Model answer: "I've visited many countries in Europe. I've tried sushi and I loved it. I've seen the Eiffel Tower. I've met some famous people. I've learned to drive."

EXERCISE 2

Talk about things you have NEVER done.

Use: I have never + past participle / I've never...

Model answer: "I've never been to Asia. I've never tried skydiving. I've never seen the Northern Lights. I've never eaten snails. I've never broken a bone."

EXERCISE 3

What are 5 interesting things you have done in your life?

Think about: travel, food, sports, achievements, people you've met.

Section 2: Recent Events (Just, Already, Yet)

EXERCISE 4

Talk about things you have just done.

Use: I've just + past participle (= hace muy poco tiempo)

Model answer: "I've just finished my coffee. I've just received a message. I've just woken up. I've just arrived home. I've just had lunch."

EXERCISE 5

Talk about things you have already done today.

Use: I've already + past participle (= ya lo he hecho)

Model answer: "I've already had breakfast. I've already checked my emails. I've already exercised. I've already made my bed. I've already called my mother."

EXERCISE 6

Talk about things you haven't done yet today.

Use: I haven't + past participle + yet (= todavía no)

Model answer: "I haven't had lunch yet. I haven't finished my work yet. I haven't called my friend yet. I haven't done the shopping yet. I haven't watched the news yet."

Section 3: Duration with For and Since

EXERCISE 7

Talk about how long you have done things (FOR + period).

For: two years, a long time, six months, ages...

Model answer: "I've studied English for three years. I've lived in this city for ten years. I've known my best friend for ages. I've worked here for six months. I've had my car for two years."

EXERCISE 8

Talk about when things started (SINCE + point in time).

Since: 2020, Monday, January, I was a child, last summer...

Model answer: "I've lived here since 2015. I've been interested in music since I was a child. I've had this phone since last year. I've been married since 2018. I haven't seen him since Christmas."

EXERCISE 9

Answer these questions using FOR or SINCE:

  • How long have you lived in your current home?
  • How long have you studied English?
  • How long have you known your best friend?
  • How long have you had your current job/studies?

Section 4: Present Perfect Continuous

EXERCISE 10

Talk about activities you have been doing recently.

Use: I've been + -ing (emphasis on the activity)

Model answer: "I've been studying hard for my exams. I've been working on a new project. I've been learning to cook Italian food. I've been reading a really good book. I've been trying to exercise more."

EXERCISE 11

Explain why you look tired/happy/different using Present Perfect Continuous.

Example: "I look tired because I've been working all night."

Model answers:

  • "I'm tired because I've been studying all day."
  • "My eyes are red because I've been crying."
  • "I'm happy because I've been talking to my friend."
  • "I'm sweating because I've been running."

Section 5: Have You Ever...?

EXERCISE 12

Ask and answer "Have you ever...?" questions.

Practice with these topics: travel, food, extreme sports, famous people, accidents.

Practice these questions and give full answers:

  • "Have you ever been to London?" → "Yes, I have. I went there in 2019." / "No, I haven't, but I'd love to go."
  • "Have you ever tried Japanese food?"
  • "Have you ever met anyone famous?"
  • "Have you ever lost your wallet?"
  • "Have you ever broken anything valuable?"
EXERCISE 13

Create 5 "Have you ever...?" questions to ask a classmate.

Section 6: Changes and Achievements

EXERCISE 14

Talk about how things have changed.

My city has changed... Technology has improved... The weather has become...

Model answer: "My city has changed a lot in recent years. Many new buildings have appeared. Technology has improved dramatically. Prices have increased. People's habits have changed."

EXERCISE 15

Talk about your achievements this year.

Use: I've managed to..., I've succeeded in..., I've achieved..., I've completed...

Model answer: "This year, I've improved my English a lot. I've finished reading five books. I've started a new hobby. I've learned to cook new recipes. I've saved some money."

Section 7: Extended Monologue (2 minutes)

EXERCISE 16 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "My life experiences"

Speak for 2 minutes about your life experiences. Include:

  • Places you have visited
  • Things you have tried
  • Things you have never done but want to
  • How long you have been doing certain things
EXERCISE 17 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "What I have been doing recently"

Describe your recent activities and projects.

Structure: "Recently, I've been very busy. I've been working on... I've also been trying to... I've been spending a lot of time... I haven't had time to... yet, but I've already..."

EXERCISE 18 - MONOLOGUE

Topic: "How my life has changed in the last 5 years"

Section 8: Role-Play Scenarios

EXERCISE 19 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Job interview about your experience

Answer these interview questions:

  • "Have you ever worked in a team?"
  • "What have you achieved in your current role?"
  • "How long have you been working in this field?"
  • "Have you ever had to solve a difficult problem?"
EXERCISE 20 - ROLE-PLAY

Scenario: Catching up with an old friend

You haven't seen a friend for years. They ask: "So, what have you been up to?"

Section 9: Quick Response Drills

QUICK DRILL

Answer these questions quickly:

  1. Have you had breakfast today?
  2. How long have you been awake?
  3. Have you ever been to the UK?
  4. What have you been doing this morning?
  5. Have you finished your homework yet?
  6. How many books have you read this year?
  7. Have you ever lost something important?
  8. What have you learned recently?
  9. Have you seen any good films lately?
  10. How long have you known your best friend?

Section 10: Spot and Correct the Error

ERROR CORRECTION

Say each sentence correctly:

  1. "I have went to Paris." → ?
  2. "She has lived here since five years." → ?
  3. "Did you ever try sushi?" → ?
  4. "I've been knowing him for years." → ?
  5. "He hasn't finished already." → ?
  6. "I live here for ten years." → ?
  7. "Have you ever went skiing?" → ?
  8. "I've been to London last year." → ?

Correct answers:

  1. "I have been to Paris." / "I have gone to Paris."
  2. "She has lived here for five years."
  3. "Have you ever tried sushi?"
  4. "I've known him for years."
  5. "He hasn't finished yet."
  6. "I've lived here for ten years."
  7. "Have you ever been skiing?"
  8. "I went to London last year."
CONSEJO

El Present Perfect es difícil porque no existe en español. Practica pensando: "¿Tiene esto conexión con el presente?" Si sí, usa Present Perfect.
