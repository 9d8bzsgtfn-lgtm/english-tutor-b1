Exercise 1: FOR or SINCE?

1. I've lived here ________ 2015.

for
since

Correct! 2015 is a point in time (when it started), so we use SINCE.

2. She's been waiting ________ two hours.

for
since

Correct! "Two hours" is a duration (period of time), so we use FOR.

3. They've known each other ________ they were children.

for
since

Correct! "When they were children" is a point in time, so we use SINCE.

Exercise 2: JUST, ALREADY, or YET?

4. Have you finished your homework ________?

just
already
yet

Correct! YET goes at the end of questions and negative sentences.

5. I've ________ sent the email. It left 2 seconds ago.

just
already
yet

Correct! JUST means "very recently" - 2 seconds ago is very recent!

6. She's ________ finished - she was faster than expected.

just
already
yet

Correct! ALREADY emphasizes "sooner than expected".

Exercise 3: Present Perfect or Past Simple?

7. I ________ to Paris three times.

have been
went
was

Correct! No specific time mentioned = talking about experience = Present Perfect.

8. I ________ to Paris last summer.

have been
went
have gone

Correct! "Last summer" is a specific time in the past = Past Simple.

9. ________ you ever ________ sushi?

Have / tried
Did / try
Have / try

Correct! EVER + experience = Present Perfect (Have + past participle).

10. She ________ here since Monday.

has been
is
was

Correct! SINCE always requires Present Perfect.
