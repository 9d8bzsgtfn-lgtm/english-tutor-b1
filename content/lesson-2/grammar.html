Grammar: Past Simple vs Past Continuous

IMPRESCINDIBLE - MEMORIZAR

Regla fundamental

Tiempo Uso Ejemplo
Past Simple Acciones completadas en el pasado I watched a film yesterday.
Past Continuous Acciones en progreso en un momento del pasado I was watching a film at 9pm.
TRUCO PARA RECORDAR

SIMPLE = Acción terminada (empezó y acabó)

CONTINUOUS = Acción en progreso (como una foto en movimiento)

Estructura gramatical

Past Simple Past Continuous
+ I/You/He/She/It/We/They worked
+ I/You/He/She/It/We/They went (irregular) 		+ I/He/She/It was working
+ You/We/They were working
- I/You/He/She/It/We/They didn't work - I/He/She/It wasn't working
- You/We/They weren't working
? Did you work? ? Were you working?
? Was he working?

Uso combinado: WHEN y WHILE

MUY IMPORTANTE PARA EXAMEN
Estructura Ejemplo Explicación
WHEN + Past Simple When the phone rang, I was sleeping. WHEN introduce la acción corta que interrumpe
WHILE + Past Continuous While I was sleeping, the phone rang. WHILE introduce la acción larga de fondo
Dos acciones simultáneas While I was cooking, she was reading. Ambas en Continuous = paralelas

Errores comunes a evitar

ERRORES FRECUENTES EN EXAMEN
Incorrecto Correcto Por qué
I was go to the cinema. I went to the cinema. Acción completa = Past Simple
When I was sleeping, the phone was ringing. When I was sleeping, the phone rang. La interrupción va en Simple
Did you went? Did you go? Con DID no se usa el pasado
I didn't went. I didn't go. Con DIDN'T no se usa el pasado
RESUMEN "SUBE LAS ANTENAS"
  • Past Simple: acciones terminadas + yesterday, ago, last...
  • Past Continuous: acciones en progreso + at that moment, while...
  • WHEN + Past Simple (acción corta/interrupción)
  • WHILE + Past Continuous (acción larga/fondo)
  • DID/DIDN'T + infinitivo (sin -ed)
