Grammar: Reported Speech

IMPRESCINDIBLE - MEMORIZAR

Direct Speech vs Reported Speech

Tipo Descripcion Ejemplo
Direct Speech Palabras exactas (con comillas) She said, "I am tired."
Reported Speech Lo que alguien dijo (sin comillas) She said that she was tired.

Uso: El Reported Speech se usa para contar lo que otra persona dijo, sin usar sus palabras exactas.

Backshift: Cambios de tiempo verbal

MEMORIZAR - BACKSHIFT

Cuando el verbo introductor esta en pasado (said, told, asked...), los tiempos verbales retroceden un paso:

Direct Speech Reported Speech Ejemplo
Present Simple Past Simple "I work here." → He said he worked there.
Present Continuous Past Continuous "I am studying." → She said she was studying.
Present Perfect Past Perfect "I have finished." → He said he had finished.
Past Simple Past Perfect "I saw her." → He said he had seen her.
Will Would "I will call you." → She said she would call me.
Can Could "I can swim." → He said he could swim.
May Might "I may go." → She said she might go.
Must Had to "I must leave." → He said he had to leave.
EXCEPCION

No hay backshift cuando:

  • El verbo introductor esta en presente: He says he is tired.
  • La informacion sigue siendo verdad: She said the Earth is round.
  • Ya esta en Past Perfect, would, could, might, should (no cambian mas)

Reported Statements: SAY vs TELL

SAY vs TELL
Verbo Estructura Ejemplo
SAY say (that) + clause He said (that) he was busy.
TELL tell + person + (that) + clause He told me (that) he was busy.

Recuerda: TELL siempre necesita un objeto (persona): told me, told her, told them

THAT es opcional y se puede omitir.

Reported Questions

PREGUNTAS REPORTADAS

Reglas importantes:

  • Usamos asked (no said)
  • NO hay inversion sujeto-verbo
  • NO hay signo de interrogacion
Yes/No Questions:

Usamos if o whether

Direct Reported
"Are you coming?" She asked if/whether I was coming.
"Do you like coffee?" He asked me if I liked coffee.
"Have you finished?" She asked whether I had finished.
Wh- Questions:

Mantenemos la question word (what, where, when, why, how, who...)

Direct Reported
"Where do you live?" He asked me where I lived.
"What time is it?" She asked what time it was.
"Why did you leave?" He asked why I had left.

Reported Commands and Requests

ORDENES Y PETICIONES

Estructura: verb + person + (not) to + infinitive

Tipo Direct Reported
Command (+) "Open the door!" He told me to open the door.
Command (-) "Don't touch that!" She told them not to touch that.
Request (+) "Please help me." He asked me to help him.
Request (-) "Please don't be late." She asked us not to be late.

Verbos comunes: told, asked, ordered, commanded, begged, warned, advised, encouraged, reminded

Otros Reporting Verbs

REPORTING VERBS + ESTRUCTURA
Verbo Estructura Ejemplo
admit admit + -ing / that He admitted stealing the money. / He admitted that he had stolen...
deny deny + -ing She denied breaking the window.
promise promise + to infinitive He promised to call me.
offer offer + to infinitive She offered to help us.
refuse refuse + to infinitive He refused to answer.
suggest suggest + -ing / that She suggested going to the cinema.
recommend recommend + -ing / that He recommended trying the soup.
warn warn + person + (not) to She warned me not to go there.
advise advise + person + to He advised me to see a doctor.
remind remind + person + to She reminded him to lock the door.

Errores comunes a evitar

ERRORES FRECUENTES
Incorrecto Correcto Por que
He said me that... He told me that... / He said that... SAY no lleva objeto directo de persona
He told that... He told me that... / He said that... TELL necesita objeto (persona)
She asked where was I. She asked where I was. No hay inversion en reported questions
He asked did I like it? He asked if I liked it. No auxiliar, no interrogacion
She told me don't go. She told me not to go. Commands: (not) to + infinitive
He said he will come. He said he would come. Backshift: will → would
RESUMEN "SUBE LAS ANTENAS"
  • Backshift: Los tiempos retroceden (present → past, will → would, etc.)
  • SAY: sin objeto de persona / TELL: con objeto de persona
  • Yes/No questions: if/whether + orden normal
  • Wh- questions: question word + orden normal (sin inversion)
  • Commands: told/asked + person + (not) to + infinitive
  • Cambios: pronombres, tiempo y lugar se adaptan al nuevo contexto
