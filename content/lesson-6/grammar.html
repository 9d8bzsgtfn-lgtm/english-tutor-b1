Grammar: Modal Verbs

IMPRESCINDIBLE - MEMORIZAR

Regla fundamental de los modales

Caracteristica Regla Ejemplo
Estructura Modal + infinitivo SIN TO She can swim. (NO: can to swim)
Negativo Modal + NOT You mustn't smoke here.
Pregunta Modal + sujeto + verbo Can you help me?
Sin -s Igual para todas las personas He can (NO: he cans)

CAN / CAN'T - Habilidad y Permiso

CAN - USOS PRINCIPALES
Uso Ejemplo Significado
Habilidad (presente) I can speak three languages. Puedo/Soy capaz de hablar
Permiso (informal) Can I use your phone? ¿Puedo usar tu telefono?
Posibilidad (general) It can be very cold in winter. Puede hacer mucho frio
Peticiones Can you help me, please? ¿Puedes ayudarme?

CAN'T = no poder, imposibilidad

I can't swim. / You can't park here.

COULD / COULDN'T - Pasado, Posibilidad y Cortesia

COULD - USOS PRINCIPALES
Uso Ejemplo Significado
Habilidad pasada I could ride a bike when I was 5. Podia/Sabia montar
Posibilidad It could rain later. Podria llover
Peticiones educadas Could you open the window? ¿Podria abrir la ventana?
Sugerencias We could go to the cinema. Podriamos ir al cine
Permiso formal Could I speak to the manager? ¿Podria hablar con...?

COULDN'T = no pude, no podia

I couldn't find my keys yesterday.

MUST / MUSTN'T - Obligacion y Prohibicion

MUST - MUY IMPORTANTE
Uso Ejemplo Significado
Obligacion personal I must finish this report today. Debo/Tengo que terminar
Reglas/normas fuertes You must wear a seatbelt. Debes ponerte el cinturon
Deduccion logica She must be tired after the trip. Debe de estar cansada
Recomendacion fuerte You must try this restaurant! ¡Tienes que probar este restaurante!
MUSTN'T vs DON'T HAVE TO
Modal Significado Ejemplo
MUSTN'T PROHIBICION (no debes) You mustn't smoke here. (Esta prohibido)
DON'T HAVE TO NO ES NECESARIO (no tienes que) You don't have to come. (Puedes venir si quieres)

¡CUIDADO! Esta diferencia es MUY importante en el examen B1.

SHOULD / SHOULDN'T - Consejo

SHOULD - CONSEJOS
Uso Ejemplo Significado
Dar consejo You should see a doctor. Deberias ver a un medico
Pedir consejo What should I do? ¿Que deberia hacer?
Opinion The government should help more. El gobierno deberia ayudar mas
Critica (pasado) You should have told me earlier. Deberias haberme dicho antes

SHOULDN'T = no deberias (consejo negativo)

You shouldn't eat so much sugar.

MAY / MIGHT - Posibilidad

MAY / MIGHT - POSIBILIDAD
Uso Ejemplo Probabilidad
MAY (posibilidad) It may rain tomorrow. Quiza llueva (~50%)
MIGHT (posibilidad menor) She might come to the party. Quiza venga (~30%)
MAY (permiso formal) May I come in? ¿Puedo entrar? (muy formal)
MAY NOT He may not know the answer. Quiza no sepa la respuesta
MIGHT NOT They might not be at home. Puede que no esten en casa

Nota: MAY y MIGHT son muy similares. MIGHT indica menor probabilidad.

HAVE TO vs MUST

DIFERENCIA CLAVE
Modal Tipo de obligacion Ejemplo
MUST Obligacion INTERNA (yo decido) I must call my mum. (Yo siento que debo)
HAVE TO Obligacion EXTERNA (reglas, otros) I have to wear a uniform. (La empresa lo exige)
HAD TO Obligacion en PASADO I had to work late yesterday.
WILL HAVE TO Obligacion en FUTURO You will have to study harder.

Importante: MUST solo tiene presente. Para pasado/futuro usa HAVE TO.

Escala de probabilidad

PROBABILIDAD CON MODALES
Certeza Modal Ejemplo
100% seguro will She will be there.
~95% muy probable must He must be at work.
~50% posible may It may be true.
~30% menos probable might / could She might call later.
~95% casi imposible can't That can't be true!

Errores comunes a evitar

ERRORES FRECUENTES
Incorrecto Correcto Por que
He can to swim. He can swim. Modal + infinitivo SIN TO
She cans speak French. She can speak French. Los modales NO llevan -s
I must to go. I must go. Modal + infinitivo SIN TO
I musted work yesterday. I had to work yesterday. MUST no tiene pasado
You mustn't to smoke. You mustn't smoke. Modal + infinitivo SIN TO
You don't must go. You don't have to go. No es necesario = don't have to
RESUMEN "SUBE LAS ANTENAS"
  • CAN: habilidad presente, permiso informal
  • COULD: habilidad pasada, posibilidad, peticiones educadas
  • MUST: obligacion personal, prohibicion (mustn't)
  • HAVE TO: obligacion externa, pasado (had to)
  • SHOULD: consejos y recomendaciones
  • MAY/MIGHT: posibilidad (might = menos probable)
  • MUSTN'T: prohibicion / DON'T HAVE TO: no necesario
  • Modal + verbo SIN TO (excepto have to, ought to)
