Practice: Modal Verbs

INSTRUCCIONES

Elige el modal correcto para completar cada oracion.

Exercise 1: CAN / COULD

1. When I was a child, I ________ run very fast.

can
could
must

Correct! COULD is used for past abilities - "When I was a child" indicates the past.

2. ________ you help me with this exercise, please?

Must
Could
Should

Correct! COULD is used for polite requests. CAN would also be correct but less formal.

Exercise 2: MUST / MUSTN'T / DON'T HAVE TO

3. You ________ smoke in the hospital. It's strictly prohibited.

don't have to
mustn't
shouldn't

Correct! MUSTN'T expresses prohibition. "Strictly prohibited" tells us it's forbidden, not optional.

4. It's Saturday. I ________ go to work today.

don't have to
mustn't
can't

Correct! DON'T HAVE TO means it's not necessary. It's the weekend, so there's no obligation to work.

5. You ________ be on time for the interview. It's very important!

must
might
could

Correct! MUST expresses strong obligation. "Very important" shows this is essential.

Exercise 3: SHOULD / SHOULDN'T

6. You look tired. You ________ go to bed earlier.

should
must
have to

Correct! SHOULD is used for giving advice. This is a suggestion, not an obligation.

7. You ________ eat so much junk food. It's bad for your health.

don't have to
shouldn't
might not

Correct! SHOULDN'T is used for negative advice - recommending not to do something.

Exercise 4: MAY / MIGHT

8. Take an umbrella. It ________ rain later.

must
might
should

Correct! MIGHT expresses possibility. We're not sure if it will rain, but it's possible.

9. Excuse me, ________ I ask you a question?

may
must
should

Correct! MAY is used for formal permission requests. "Excuse me" indicates formality.

Exercise 5: Mixed modals

10. He's been working for 12 hours. He ________ be exhausted!

must
should
may

Correct! MUST is used for logical deductions when we're almost certain. 12 hours of work = very tired!
