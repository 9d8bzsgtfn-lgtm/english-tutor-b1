Practice: Relative Clauses

INSTRUCCIONES

Completa con el pronombre relativo correcto (who, which, that, whose, where, when) o decide si se puede omitir.

Exercise 1: Choose the correct relative pronoun

1. The woman ________ lives next door is a doctor.

who
which
whose

Correct! We use WHO for people. "Who" is the subject of "lives".

2. The book ________ I'm reading is very interesting.

who
which / that
where

Correct! We use WHICH or THAT for things. Note: we could also omit it here because it's the object.

3. That's the restaurant ________ we had dinner last week.

which
where
when

Correct! We use WHERE for places. "Where we had dinner" = "at which we had dinner".

4. The man ________ car was stolen called the police.

who
which
whose

Correct! We use WHOSE to show possession. "Whose car" = "his car".

5. I remember the day ________ we first met.

which
where
when

Correct! We use WHEN for time expressions. "The day when" refers to a point in time.

Exercise 2: Defining or Non-defining?

6. My sister ________ lives in Paris is coming to visit. (I have only one sister)

who (no commas)
, who (with commas)
that

Correct! Since you have only one sister, the information is extra (non-defining), so we need commas. We cannot use THAT in non-defining clauses.

7. Students ________ study hard usually get good results.

who / that (no commas)
, who (with commas)
which

Correct! This is a defining clause - we're specifying WHICH students (only those who study hard). No commas needed.

Exercise 3: Can we omit the pronoun?

8. The film ________ we watched last night was boring. Can we omit the pronoun?

Yes - "The film we watched..."
No - pronoun is required

Correct! We can omit WHICH/THAT because it's the object of "watched". "We" is the subject, so the pronoun is optional.

9. The girl ________ won the prize is my cousin. Can we omit the pronoun?

Yes - "The girl won the prize..."
No - "who" is the subject

Correct! We CANNOT omit WHO because it's the subject of "won". Without it, the sentence doesn't make sense.

10. The Eiffel Tower, ________ is in Paris, is very famous. Which is correct?

which (with commas)
that (with commas)
which (no commas)

Correct! This is non-defining (extra information about a specific, known thing). We must use WHICH with commas. THAT is never used in non-defining clauses.
