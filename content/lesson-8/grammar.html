Grammar: Passive Voice

IMPRESCINDIBLE - MEMORIZAR

Estructura fundamental

Voz Estructura Ejemplo
Active Subject + verb + object Shakespeare wrote Hamlet.
Passive Subject + BE + past participle Hamlet was written by Shakespeare.

Fórmula clave: BE (conjugado) + PAST PARTICIPLE (3ª columna verbos irregulares)

La pasiva en diferentes tiempos verbales

TIEMPOS VERBALES
Tiempo Estructura pasiva Ejemplo
Present Simple am/is/are + past participle Coffee is grown in Brazil.
Past Simple was/were + past participle The car was stolen last night.
Future Simple will be + past participle The results will be announced tomorrow.
Present Perfect have/has been + past participle The letter has been sent.
Past Perfect had been + past participle The house had been sold before we arrived.
Present Continuous am/is/are being + past participle The road is being repaired.
Past Continuous was/were being + past participle The car was being fixed when I called.
Modal verbs modal + be + past participle This must be done today.

Cuándo usar la voz pasiva

USOS DE LA PASIVA
Situación Ejemplo Por qué pasiva
El agente es desconocido My bike was stolen. No sabemos quién lo robó
El agente es obvio He was arrested. Obvio: la policía
El agente no importa The pyramids were built 4,500 years ago. El enfoque está en las pirámides
Énfasis en la acción/resultado A new hospital has been opened. Lo importante es el hospital
Contextos formales/científicos The experiment was conducted in 2023. Estilo objetivo
Noticias y titulares Three people were injured in the accident. Enfoque en las víctimas

BY + Agent

EL AGENTE

Usamos BY + agente solo cuando es información relevante o importante:

Con BY (agente importante) Sin BY (agente no importante)
The Mona Lisa was painted by Leonardo da Vinci. The window was broken.
This song was written by John Lennon. English is spoken all over the world.
The company was founded by Steve Jobs. The meeting has been cancelled.

Regla: Si el agente es "people", "someone", "they" (genérico) = NO ponemos BY

Transformación: Activa a Pasiva

TRANSFORMACIÓN PASO A PASO

Activa: The chef prepares the food every day.

  1. El objeto de la activa se convierte en sujeto de la pasiva: The food
  2. Conjugar BE en el mismo tiempo: is (present simple)
  3. Añadir el participio pasado: prepared
  4. Añadir BY + agente (si es relevante): by the chef

Pasiva: The food is prepared by the chef every day.

Active Passive
Someone stole my wallet. My wallet was stolen.
They will build a new bridge. A new bridge will be built.
People speak Spanish in Mexico. Spanish is spoken in Mexico.
The company has hired 50 workers. 50 workers have been hired.

Pasiva con verbos modales

MODAL + BE + PAST PARTICIPLE
Modal Active Passive
can You can see the mountains from here. The mountains can be seen from here.
must You must finish this today. This must be finished today.
should They should clean the room. The room should be cleaned.
might Someone might find the answer. The answer might be found.
have to You have to do the homework. The homework has to be done.

Errores comunes a evitar

ERRORES FRECUENTES
Incorrecto Correcto Por qué
The car was repair. The car was repaired. Falta el PAST PARTICIPLE
The book written by Cervantes. The book was written by Cervantes. Falta el verbo BE
It was happened yesterday. It happened yesterday. HAPPEN es intransitivo (no tiene pasiva)
The thief was caught from the police. The thief was caught by the police. El agente usa BY, no FROM
Spanish is speak in Spain. Spanish is spoken in Spain. Usar PARTICIPIO, no infinitivo
The letter has been send. The letter has been sent. SEND es irregular: send-sent-sent

Verbos que NO tienen pasiva

VERBOS INTRANSITIVOS

Estos verbos no tienen objeto, por lo tanto no pueden formar pasiva:

  • happen - The accident happened yesterday. (NO: was happened)
  • arrive - The train arrived late. (NO: was arrived)
  • sleep - I slept well. (NO: was slept)
  • die - He died in 1990. (NO: was died)
  • exist - Dinosaurs existed millions of years ago.
RESUMEN "SUBE LAS ANTENAS"
  • Pasiva: BE + PAST PARTICIPLE
  • Present: is/are + done | Past: was/were + done
  • Future: will be + done | Perfect: have/has been + done
  • BY + agente: solo si es información relevante
  • Modales: modal + be + participio (can be done, must be done)
  • No pasiva: verbos intransitivos (happen, arrive, die...)
