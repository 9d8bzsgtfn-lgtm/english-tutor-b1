# B1 Grammar Reference

Complete grammar guide for B1 level English learners.

---

## 1. Verb Tenses

### 1.1 Present Simple

**Uso:** Hábitos, rutinas, verdades generales, horarios fijos

**Estructura:**
```
Afirmativa: Subject + verb (+ s/es for he/she/it)
Negativa:   Subject + don't/doesn't + verb
Pregunta:   Do/Does + subject + verb?
```

**Ejemplos:**
- I **work** from home every Monday.
- She **doesn't like** coffee.
- **Do** you **speak** French?

**Marcadores temporales:** always, usually, often, sometimes, rarely, never, every day/week/month

**Reglas de tercera persona (he/she/it):**
- Añadir -s: work → works
- Añadir -es (verbos en -s, -sh, -ch, -x, -o): watch → watches, go → goes
- Consonante + y → -ies: study → studies

---

### 1.2 Present Continuous

**Uso:** Acciones en progreso ahora, situaciones temporales, planes futuros confirmados

**Estructura:**
```
Afirmativa: Subject + am/is/are + verb-ing
Negativa:   Subject + am/is/are + not + verb-ing
Pregunta:   Am/Is/Are + subject + verb-ing?
```

**Ejemplos:**
- I **am working** on a new project.
- She **isn't listening** to me.
- **Are** you **coming** to the party tonight?

**Marcadores temporales:** now, at the moment, currently, today, this week

**Formación del -ing:**
- Regla general: añadir -ing: work → working
- Termina en -e: quitar -e: make → making
- Consonante corta + vocal + consonante: doblar consonante: run → running
- Termina en -ie: cambiar a -y: die → dying

---

### 1.3 Present Simple vs Present Continuous

| Present Simple | Present Continuous |
|----------------|-------------------|
| Hábitos y rutinas | Acciones temporales |
| Verdades generales | Acciones en progreso |
| Horarios fijos | Situaciones cambiantes |

**Comparación:**
- I **work** in a bank. (es mi trabajo)
- I **am working** from home today. (solo hoy)

---

### 1.4 State Verbs (Verbos de Estado)

Estos verbos **NO se usan normalmente en tiempos continuos**:

| Categoría | Verbos |
|-----------|--------|
| Sentidos | see, hear, smell, taste, feel |
| Emociones | love, hate, like, prefer, want |
| Mente | know, believe, understand, remember, think (opinar) |
| Posesión | have (tener), own, belong, possess |
| Otros | need, cost, mean, contain |

**Correcto:** I **know** the answer.
**Incorrecto:** ~~I am knowing the answer.~~

**Excepción:** Algunos verbos cambian de significado:
- I **think** it's good. (opino)
- I **am thinking** about it. (estoy reflexionando)

---

### 1.5 Past Simple

```
Pregunta:   Did + subject + verb?
```

**Ejemplos:**
- I **visited** Paris last year.
- She **didn't go** to the meeting.
- **Did** you **see** that movie?

**Marcadores temporales:** yesterday, last week/month/year, ago, in 2020, when I was young

### 1.6 Past Continuous

**Uso:** Acciones en progreso en un momento del pasado, contexto/trasfondo de una historia

**Estructura:**
```
Afirmativa: Subject + was/were + verb-ing
Negativa:   Subject + wasn't/weren't + verb-ing
Pregunta:   Was/Were + subject + verb-ing?
```

**Ejemplos:**
- I **was watching** TV when you called.
- They **weren't listening** to the teacher.
- **Were** you **sleeping** at 10pm?

**Uso con Past Simple:**
- **While** I **was walking** home, I **met** an old friend.
- I **was cooking** dinner **when** the phone **rang**.

**Regla:**
- Past Continuous = acción larga/contexto
- Past Simple = acción corta que interrumpe

---

### 1.7 Used to

**Uso:** Hábitos o estados pasados que ya no existen

**Estructura:**
```
Afirmativa: Subject + used to + verb
Negativa:   Subject + didn't use to + verb
Pregunta:   Did + subject + use to + verb?
```

**Ejemplos:**
- I **used to live** in Madrid. (Ya no vivo allí)
- She **didn't use to like** vegetables. (Ahora le gustan)
- **Did** you **use to play** football? (¿Antes jugabas?)

**Diferencia con Past Simple:**
- I **used to smoke**. (era un hábito continuo)
- I **smoked** a cigarette yesterday. (una acción específica)

---

### 1.8 Present Perfect Simple

**Uso:** Experiencias de vida, acciones pasadas con relevancia presente, acciones recientes

**Estructura:**
```
Afirmativa: Subject + have/has + past participle
Negativa:   Subject + haven't/hasn't + past participle
Pregunta:   Have/Has + subject + past participle?
```

**Ejemplos:**
- I **have visited** France three times.
- She **has never tried** sushi.
- **Have** you **finished** your homework?

**Marcadores temporales:**
- **ever** (alguna vez): Have you **ever** been to Japan?
- **never** (nunca): I've **never** seen that movie.
- **already** (ya): I've **already** eaten.
- **yet** (todavía - preguntas y negativas): Have you finished **yet**?
- **just** (acaba de): She's **just** arrived.
- **for** (durante + período): I've lived here **for** 5 years.
- **since** (desde + momento): I've worked here **since** 2020.

---

### 1.9 Present Perfect vs Past Simple

| Present Perfect | Past Simple |
|-----------------|-------------|
| Sin tiempo específico | Con tiempo específico |
| Relevante al presente | Completamente pasado |
| Experiencia de vida | Acción única pasada |

**Ejemplos:**
- I **have been** to Paris. (experiencia, no importa cuándo)
- I **went** to Paris last year. (tiempo específico)

- I **have lost** my keys. (todavía no las tengo)
- I **lost** my keys yesterday. (acción pasada)

---

### 1.10 Future: Will

**Uso:** Predicciones, decisiones espontáneas, promesas, ofrecimientos

**Estructura:**
```
Afirmativa: Subject + will + verb
Negativa:   Subject + won't + verb
Pregunta:   Will + subject + verb?
```

**Ejemplos:**
- I think it **will rain** tomorrow. (predicción)
- I'**ll help** you with that. (ofrecimiento)
- I **won't tell** anyone. (promesa)
- I'**ll have** the chicken, please. (decisión espontánea)

---

### 1.11 Future: Going to

**Uso:** Planes e intenciones, predicciones basadas en evidencia

**Estructura:**
```
Afirmativa: Subject + am/is/are + going to + verb
Negativa:   Subject + am/is/are + not + going to + verb
Pregunta:   Am/Is/Are + subject + going to + verb?
```

**Ejemplos:**
- I **'m going to study** medicine. (plan/intención)
- Look at those clouds! It **'s going to rain**. (evidencia)
- She **isn't going to come** to the party. (plan negativo)

---

### 1.12 Will vs Going to

| Will | Going to |
|------|----------|
| Decisiones espontáneas | Planes previos |
| Predicciones (opinión) | Predicciones (evidencia) |
| Promesas y ofrecimientos | Intenciones |

**Ejemplos:**
- "The phone is ringing." "I**'ll get** it." (espontáneo)
- I**'m going to visit** my parents next weekend. (planeado)

---

### 1.13 Present Continuous for Future

**Uso:** Planes y citas confirmados/organizados

**Estructura:** Igual que Present Continuous

**Ejemplos:**
- I **'m meeting** John at 3pm tomorrow. (cita confirmada)
- We **'re flying** to New York next Monday. (vuelo reservado)
- **Are** you **doing** anything tonight?

**Diferencia con Going to:**
- I'**m meeting** Sarah tomorrow. (está organizado, quizás hay lugar y hora)
- I'**m going to meet** Sarah tomorrow. (es mi intención, puede no estar cerrado)

## 2. Modal Verbs

### 2.1 Can / Could / Be able to

**Habilidad:**
- I **can** speak French. (presente)
- I **could** swim when I was 5. (pasado)
- I **will be able to** help you tomorrow. (futuro)

**Permiso:**
- **Can** I use your phone?
- **Could** I borrow your pen? (más formal)

**Posibilidad:**
- It **could** rain later.
- That **can't** be true!

---

### 2.2 Must / Have to

**Obligación:**
- I **must** finish this today. (obligación personal)
- I **have to** wear a uniform. (obligación externa)

**Prohibición:**
- You **mustn't** smoke here. (prohibido)

**Deducción:**
- She **must** be tired. (seguro que está cansada)

**Diferencia:**
- **Must:** obligación interna, el hablante lo impone
- **Have to:** obligación externa, las reglas lo imponen

---

### 2.3 Should / Ought to / Had better

**Consejo:**
- You **should** see a doctor.
- You **ought to** exercise more.
- You'**d better** hurry up. (más urgente)

**Expectativa:**
- The bus **should** arrive at 10.

**Crítica (pasado):**
- You **should have told** me! (¡Deberías habérmelo dicho!)

---

### 2.4 May / Might

**Posibilidad:**
- It **may** rain later. (50% posible)
- It **might** rain later. (menos seguro)

**Permiso (formal):**
- **May** I come in?

---

## 3. Conditionals

### 3.1 Zero Conditional

**Uso:** Verdades generales, hechos científicos

**Estructura:**
```
If + present simple, present simple
```

**Ejemplos:**
- If you heat water to 100°C, it boils.
- If I drink coffee at night, I can't sleep.

---

### 3.2 First Conditional

**Uso:** Situaciones posibles/probables en el futuro

**Estructura:**
```
If + present simple, will + verb
```

**Ejemplos:**
- If it rains, I **will stay** home.
- If you study, you **will pass** the exam.
- I **won't go** if you don't come with me.

**Variaciones:**
- If + present, modal verb: If you're tired, you **should** rest.
- If + present, imperative: If you see John, **tell** him to call me.

---

### 3.3 Second Conditional

**Uso:** Situaciones hipotéticas/improbables, imaginación

**Estructura:**
```
If + past simple, would + verb
```

**Ejemplos:**
- If I **won** the lottery, I **would buy** a house.
- If I **were** you, I **would accept** the job.
- What **would** you **do** if you **had** more time?

**Nota:** Con "be", se usa "were" para todos los sujetos en lenguaje formal:
- If I **were** rich... (formal)
- If I **was** rich... (informal)

---

### 3.4 Third Conditional (Introducción)

**Uso:** Situaciones pasadas que no ocurrieron

**Estructura:**
```
If + past perfect, would have + past participle
```

**Ejemplos:**
- If I **had studied** more, I **would have passed** the exam.
- If she **had left** earlier, she **wouldn't have missed** the bus.

---

## 4. Passive Voice

### 4.1 Present Simple Passive

**Estructura:**
```
Subject + am/is/are + past participle
```

**Ejemplos:**
- Coffee **is grown** in Brazil.
- These cars **are made** in Germany.
- English **is spoken** here.

---

### 4.2 Past Simple Passive

**Estructura:**
```
Subject + was/were + past participle
```

**Ejemplos:**
- The book **was written** by Cervantes.
- The pyramids **were built** thousands of years ago.
- I **was born** in Madrid.

---

### 4.3 When to Use Passive

Usamos la voz pasiva cuando:
1. No sabemos quién hizo la acción
2. No es importante quién la hizo
3. Es obvio quién la hizo
4. Queremos enfatizar la acción, no el agente

**Activa:** Someone stole my bike.
**Pasiva:** My bike **was stolen**.

## 5. Relative Clauses

### 5.1 Defining Relative Clauses

**Uso:** Información esencial que identifica

**Pronombres:**
- **who** - personas
- **which** - cosas
- **that** - personas o cosas
- **where** - lugares
- **when** - tiempo
- **whose** - posesión

**Ejemplos:**
- The man **who lives next door** is a doctor.
- The book **which/that I bought** was expensive.
- That's the restaurant **where we met**.
- I remember the day **when I graduated**.

**Nota:** "That" puede sustituir a "who" y "which" en defining clauses:
- The woman **that** works here...
- The car **that** I bought...

---

### 5.2 Non-defining Relative Clauses

**Uso:** Información extra, no esencial (con comas)

**Ejemplos:**
- My sister, **who lives in London**, is visiting us.
- The Eiffel Tower, **which was built in 1889**, is in Paris.

**Nota:** NO se puede usar "that" en non-defining clauses.

---

## 6. Reported Speech (Introducción)

### 6.1 Reporting Statements

**Cambios de tiempo verbal:**

| Direct Speech | Reported Speech |
|---------------|-----------------|
| Present Simple | Past Simple |
| Present Continuous | Past Continuous |
| Past Simple | Past Perfect |
| Will | Would |
| Can | Could |

**Ejemplos:**
- "I am tired." → She said (that) she **was** tired.
- "I will call you." → He said he **would** call me.

---

### 6.2 Reporting Questions

**Yes/No questions:**
- "Are you coming?" → She asked **if/whether** I was coming.

**Wh- questions:**
- "Where do you live?" → He asked **where** I lived.

**Nota:** En reported questions, el orden es afirmativo (no inversión):
- ~~He asked where did I live.~~
- He asked where I **lived**. ✓

## 7. Comparatives and Superlatives

### 7.1 Comparatives

**Adjetivos cortos (1 sílaba):**
- tall → tall**er** than
- big → bigg**er** than (doblar consonante)
- nice → nic**er** than (quitar -e)

**Adjetivos de 2+ sílabas:**
- expensive → **more** expensive than
- interesting → **more** interesting than

**Adjetivos de 2 sílabas terminados en -y:**
- happy → happ**ier** than
- easy → eas**ier** than

**Irregulares:**
- good → **better** than
- bad → **worse** than
- far → **farther/further** than

---

### 7.2 Superlatives

**Adjetivos cortos:**
- tall → **the** tall**est**
- big → **the** bigg**est**

**Adjetivos largos:**
- expensive → **the most** expensive
- interesting → **the most** interesting

**Irregulares:**
- good → **the best**
- bad → **the worst**
- far → **the farthest/furthest**

---

### 7.3 Estructuras Comparativas

- **as ... as:** She is **as tall as** her brother.
- **not as ... as:** It's **not as cold as** yesterday.
- **the ... the ...:** **The more** you practice, **the better** you get.
- **much/a lot/far + comparative:** This is **much better**.
- **a bit/a little + comparative:** It's **a bit cheaper**.

## 8. Quantifiers

### 8.1 Contables vs Incontables

| Contables | Incontables |
|-----------|-------------|
| many | much |
| a few | a little |
| few | little |
| fewer | less |
| (a) number of | (an) amount of |

---

### 8.2 Uso de Quantifiers

**Afirmativas:**
- There is **a lot of / lots of** traffic. (ambos)
- There are **many / a lot of** people. (contables)
- There is **much / a lot of** water. (incontables)

**Negativas e interrogativas:**
- Is there **much** milk?
- Are there **many** students?
- There isn't **much** time.
- There aren't **many** options.

**Few vs A few:**
- **Few** = casi ninguno (negativo): Few people came. (casi nadie)
- **A few** = algunos (positivo): A few people came. (algunos vinieron)

**Little vs A little:**
- **Little** = casi nada (negativo): There's little hope.
- **A little** = algo (positivo): There's a little hope.

---

### 8.3 Too / Enough

**Too + adjective/adverb:**
- It's **too hot** to go outside.
- He drives **too fast**.

**Adjective/adverb + enough:**
- She isn't **old enough** to drive.
- He doesn't work **hard enough**.

**Enough + noun:**
- There isn't **enough time**.
- Do you have **enough money**?

## 9. Linking Words

### 9.1 Adición
- **and** - y
- **also** - también
- **as well (as)** - también, además de
- **in addition** - además
- **furthermore** - además
- **moreover** - además

### 9.2 Contraste
- **but** - pero
- **however** - sin embargo
- **although / though / even though** - aunque
- **despite / in spite of** + noun/-ing - a pesar de
- **on the other hand** - por otro lado
- **while / whereas** - mientras que

### 9.3 Causa
- **because** - porque
- **because of** + noun - debido a

### 9.4 Resultado
- **so** - así que
- **therefore** - por lo tanto
- **as a result** - como resultado
- **consequently** - en consecuencia
- **that's why** - por eso

### 9.5 Propósito
- **to + infinitive** - para
- **in order to** - para
- **so that** - para que
- **so as to** - para

## 10. Question Forms

### 10.1 Yes/No Questions

```
Auxiliary + subject + main verb?
```

- **Do** you like pizza?
- **Is** she coming?
- **Have** you finished?
- **Can** you help me?

---

### 10.2 Wh- Questions

```
Wh-word + auxiliary + subject + main verb?
```

- **What** do you do?
- **Where** does she live?
- **Why** did you leave?
- **How** can I help?

---

### 10.3 Subject Questions

Cuando "who" o "what" es el sujeto, NO hay inversión:

- **Who** called you? (no: Who did call you?)
- **What** happened? (no: What did happen?)
- **Who** lives here?

---

### 10.4 Indirect Questions

Más formales y educadas, sin inversión:

- Could you tell me **where the station is**?
- Do you know **if he's coming**?
- I was wondering **whether you could help me**.

## 11. Verb Patterns

### 11.1 Verb + to-infinitive

| Verb | Example |
|------|---------|
| want | I **want to go**. |
| need | I **need to study**. |
| decide | She **decided to leave**. |
| hope | I **hope to see** you. |
| plan | We **plan to travel**. |
| promise | He **promised to call**. |
| refuse | She **refused to help**. |
| learn | I'm **learning to drive**. |
| seem | He **seems to be** tired. |
| expect | I **expect to finish** soon. |
| would like | I**'d like to go**. |
| agree | They **agreed to meet**. |
| offer | He **offered to help**. |

---

### 11.2 Verb + -ing

| Verb | Example |
|------|---------|
| enjoy | I **enjoy reading**. |
| finish | She **finished working**. |
| mind | Do you **mind waiting**? |
| suggest | He **suggested going** out. |
| avoid | I **avoid driving** at night. |
| keep | **Keep trying**! |
| consider | I'm **considering moving**. |
| imagine | I can't **imagine living** there. |
| miss | I **miss seeing** you. |
| practise | **Practise speaking**. |
| recommend | I **recommend visiting**. |
| risk | Don't **risk losing** it. |

---

### 11.3 Verb + -ing or to-infinitive (mismo significado)

- begin, start, continue, like, love, hate, prefer

Example: I **started to learn** / I **started learning**.

---

### 11.4 Verb + -ing or to-infinitive (diferente significado)

**Remember:**
- I **remember locking** the door. (recuerdo haberlo hecho)
- **Remember to lock** the door. (acuérdate de hacerlo)

**Forget:**
- I'll never **forget meeting** her. (no olvidaré haberla conocido)
- Don't **forget to call**. (no olvides llamar)

**Stop:**
- I **stopped smoking**. (dejé de fumar)
- I **stopped to smoke**. (paré para fumar)

**Try:**
- **Try eating** less sugar. (prueba a comer menos)
- I **tried to open** the door. (intenté abrir)
